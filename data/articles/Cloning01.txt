August 3 &#8211; August 7, 2015</h1> 									 	<div class="below-title-meta"> 		<div class="adt"> 		By        <span class="author">          <a href="http://blog.loispaul.com/author/alex-murray/" title="Posts by Alex Murray" rel="author">Alex Murray</a>        </span>          <span class="meta-sep">|</span>           August 7, 2015           </div> 		 <div class="adt-comment"> 		 <a src="http://blog.loispaul.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/TWIS_6.26.jpg" alt="TWIS" width="698" height="360" /></a><a href="http://blog.loispaul.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/7_6-9-2015-TWIS.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-3583"><br /> </a>Happy Friday everyone, let&#8217;s kick the weekend off with a brand new TWIS. This week we&#8217;re covering Snapchat, Facebook&#8217;s cloning, YouTube, and Twitter contests. Don&#8217;t forget to share as always and let us know what you think in the comments!</p> <p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3696" src="http://blog.loispaul.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/enhanced-31618-1438884354-8.png" alt="enhanced-31618-1438884354-8" width="625" height="371" /></p> <p><strong>1. <a href="http://www.buzzfeed.com/alexkantrowitz/an-adults-guide-to-snapchat#.gpWNNV3GlD" target="_blank">The Adult&#8217;s Guide to Snapchat</a></strong><br /> Still confused on just what this social network has to offer? Who better than the ultimate teen source, Buzzfeed, to build the adult&#8217;s guide to Snapchat. Reporter Alex Kantrowitz explains his initial frustration with the app and his slow transition to loving it. Turns out, Snapchat is not all about the selfie. The goal of Snapchat is to convey a story through photos without having to worry about the photos existing forever or clogging up your friend&#8217;s phone. This is exemplified through the use of Snapchat stories, a feature of the app where users can share photos and videos that last 24 hours. Along with these &#8216;snaps&#8217;, media companies provide news and stories on a digestible platform while Snapchat curators share stories from around the world. Snapchat&#8217;s application is easy to see in the world of media and consumer retail but the B2B promise is yet to be seen. We recommend that B2B companies understand the platform&#8217;s capabilities, but at this point it is still too limiting to throw any real money towards it.</p> <p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3697" src="http://blog.loispaul.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/facebook-live.png" alt="facebook-live" width="738" height="387" /></p> <p><strong>2. <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2015/08/05/facescope/" target="_blank">Facebook Launches it&#8217;s Periscope Competitor </a></strong><br /> Why acquire a technology when you can just clone it? That&#8217;s always been the Facebook way of doing things. Earlier this week, the social network introduced a new feature allowing celebrities to livestream video to their fans through the platform. Although currently only for celebrities, the feature is clearly a test run for implementing it throughout the service. Potential competitors Periscope and Meerkat have already found their sweet spot in event streaming. Celebrities, along with brands, have taken advantage of livestreaming hype to share exciting stories and it will be interesting to see how Facebook&#8217;s efforts will help with the maturity of the approach.</p> <p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3698" src="http://blog.loispaul.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/Capture.png" alt="Capture" width="816" height="408" /></p> <p><strong>3. <a href="http://thenextweb.com/google/2015/08/05/youtube-is-getting-rid-of-its-weird-301-view-count/" target="_blank">YouTube Ditches the 301+</a></strong><br /> Have you ever noticed while viewing a recently uploaded video that the view count remains at 301+? YouTube&#8217;s been using this symbol for years to represent it&#8217;s robot vetting process. When a new video on YouTube receives a rush of views in a short amount of time, YouTube essentially guards the view counter from being exploited by bots by only showing &#8220;301+&#8221; as it shifts through what&#8217;s real and what&#8217;s not. Earlier this week, YouTube announced that the vetting software has become quite a bit smarter and no longer needs the safety net. If your video is lucky enough to even go remotely viral, this new feature will make metric tracking a whole lot easier.</p> <p><strong>4. <a href="http://www.hscott.net/twitter-contest-winning-as-a-service/" target="_blank">Twitter Contest Winning as a Service</a><a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/10awogKEQ_QLrkstfp30RKeBRTij4TI-PWMXkEDjD9O4/preview?sle=true" target="_blank"><br /> </a></strong>Computer Engineer Hunter Scott recently  developed a Twitter bot that automatically entered him into contests running on Twitter. After the 9 month project, Hunter averaged 4 wins a day, winning prizes spanning the gambit from concerts to tele novella merchandise. During the project, Hunter gathered some unexpected data and shared his findings in a blog post. Despite the seemingly impressive stat of 4 wins a day, Hunter entered 165,000 contests, winning 1000, rounding out to a win rate of 0.5%. Hunter&#8217;s experiment gives an interesting glimpse into the world of Twitter contests.</p> <p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3699" src="http://blog.loispaul.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/rt2win.png" alt="rt2win" width="584" height="93" /></p> <p><strong>Honorable Mention:</strong></p> <p><strong><a
