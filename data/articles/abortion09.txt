NYAAF’s board is currently an all-volunteer board responsible for all areas of NYAAF’s development and operations.</p> <p>ABOUT NYAAF</p> <p style="padding-left: 30px;">NYAAF believes every individual should be able to determine their own reproductive destiny and have access to the resources, rights, and respect to be able to do so. NYAAF’s mission is rooted in this belief: We support anyone who is unable to pay fully for an abortion and is living in or traveling to New York State by providing financial assistance and connections to other resources. We also strongly value the inclusion of those who have traditionally struggled to access reproductive health care including low-income people, immigrant communities, women of color, transgender and gender non-conforming people.</p> <p style="padding-left: 30px;"> Our operations primarily consist of working directly with callers to pledge funds toward the cost of their abortion, fundraising, and managing our growing organization’s development. We recently under went a strategic planning process and are currently working to incorporate our strategic plan. We are also in the process of transitioning our organizational model to a membership-based model. Joining the board at this exciting juncture means there is a lot of room to guide NYAAF’s direction, sustainability, and impact. Please see our 2014 Annual Report here for more information about our work.</p> <p>WHO</p> <p style="padding-left: 30px;">We seek <strong><u>Board Members</u></strong> who can contribute to our current Board of Directors in just some the following ways:</p> <ul> <li><strong>Vision and leadership</strong> (and leadership potential!)</li> <li><strong>Diversity</strong> in race, ethnicity, age, economic status/stature, parent status, ability, sexuality, gender identity, professional and career background, and background in accessing healthcare.</li> <li>Have <strong>experience, skills, know-how, and passion</strong> in any of following areas: <ul> <li>Institutional fundraising</li> <li>Event organizing</li> <li>Change management and strategic organizational development, including resource coordination</li> <li>Strategic communications</li> <li>Collective organizational structures</li> <li>Treasury skills and 501(c)(3) financial literacy and management</li> <li>Operations and Human Resources-related skills</li> <li>Incorporating anti-oppression ideas and practice into all aspects of non-profit operations</li> <li>Executive level non-profit management experience</li> </ul> </li> </ul> <p style="padding-left: 30px;">In addition to the above, we also seek a <strong><u>Transition Co-Chair</u></strong>, a specialized board member to serve on a short-term basis whose responsibilities center around seeing NYAAF through a transitional phase. Overall, responsibilities will include coordinating short and long-range planning efforts, including:</p> <ul> <li>Monitoring progress on organizational goals per NYAAF&#8217;s strategic plan including progress towards the hiring of an Executive Director and transition to a membership model.</li> <li>Ensuring the work of committees align to established timelines</li> <li>Working with Committees to identify capacity-building needs relevant to NYAAF&#8217;s transition and coordinate implementation of activities.</li> </ul> <p>HOW</p> <p style="padding-left: 30px;">Please download our application <a href="http://www.nyaaf.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/NYAAF-Board-Application_2015.FINAL4dist.2.doc">here</a>. Please email your application and resume to <a href="mailto:info@nyaaf.org">info@nyaaf.org</a> with the subject line “Board Member Application.” We will be accepting applications on a rolling basis until <strong><u>July 15th</u></strong>,<strong> but earlier applications are <em>strongly</em> encouraged! </strong>We can’t wait to get to know you!</p> <p style="padding-left: 30px;">We will be in touch with strong applicants for a <strong>casual interview and to discuss what serving on our board might look like for you</strong> and our timeline for making invitations to serve. <strong>We view this as a two-way process</strong>. Not only do we hope to find applicants that contribute to our needs, values, and mission, but we hope to be a good fit for your goals as a member of our Board of Directors.</p> <p style="padding-left: 30px;">Please direct any questions to Julie Krumwiede at <a href="mailto:Krumwiede.julie@gmail.com">Krumwiede.julie@gmail.com</a>.</p> <p>WHEN</p> <p style="padding-left: 30px;">We will be <strong>accepting applications on a rolling basis until July 15th</strong>, but earlier applications are strongly encouraged!</p> <p><em>*****Women of color, trans and gender-non-conforming people (including trans women), and people who have experienced difficulty in accessing necessary healthcare are especially encouraged to apply*****</em></p> 					</div><!-- .entry-content --> 		 		<footer class="entry-content"> 			<p>Hi! Please circulate this post to any individuals or organizations that may be interested! See text below, and find PDF version available for download <a href="https://www.scribd.com/doc/266588068/Request-for-Proposals-Racial-Justice-Trainer">here</a>. We look forward to further educating and bettering ourselves on racial justice issues and intersectionality. Thanks in advance!</p> <div class="page" title="Page 1"> <div class="layoutArea"> <div class="column"> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'Arial'; font-weight: bold; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS </span></p> </div> </div> <div class="layoutArea"> <div class="column"> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">NYAAF is seeking a consultant/firm to facilitate a one-day long racial justice training which will be supplemented with technical guidance to incorporate racial justice principles into our work. It is anticipated that the consultant/firm will provide NYAAF with political education training, an organizational assessment, and a road map for operationalizing racial justice within the Fund’s work. </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'Arial'; font-weight: bold; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">Organizational Background: </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'Arial'; font-weight: bold; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">NYAAF Statement of Values: </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">NYAAF believes that every person should be able to determine their own reproductive destiny and have access to the resources, rights, and respect to be able to do so. We believe that access to affordable abortion care is a critical component of full-spectrum healthcare services. We are working towards a world where abortion funds are not necessary because affordable, safe abortion care is part of mainstream medical practice and covered fully by all types of insurance. </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">NYAAF believes that the people who call our hotline are experts on their own lives. We believe that they do not owe us anything, including their stories or repayment, in order to qualify for abortion funding. At our core, we believe that anyone seeking an abortion has the right to access one. </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">We recognize that systems of oppression, including racism, sexism, classism, homophobia, transphobia, and others contribute to systemic injustices in the healthcare system. NYAAF is committed to recognizing and addressing these barriers to the best of our ability. </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">NYAAF’s belief in physical autonomy is a core link between our belief in the right to an abortion, and our belief in the rights of transgender and gender nonconforming people to access holistic health care and services. We recognize that people who identify as men can become pregnant and seek abortions; we strive to meet the needs of all people who seek our funding. We work to provide additional resources to callers beyond the scope of abortion funding, such as referrals for Medicaid enrollment, to demonstrate that we value the wellbeing of our callers beyond their need for an abortion. </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">NYAAF also recognizes that many of our callers would choose to continue their pregnancies if they were in different financial circumstances. As such, NYAAF does not try to speak for the motivations of our clients by framing abortion as an issue of choice. </span></p> </div> </div> <div class="layoutArea"> <div class="column"> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(16.078430%, 16.078430%, 16.078430%);">The New York Abortion Access Fund supports anyone who is unable to pay fully for an abortion and is living in or traveling to New York State by providing financial assistance and connections to other resources. </span></p> </div> </div> </div> <div class="page" title="Page 2"> <div class="layoutArea"> <div class="column"> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">Reproductive freedom is a matter of social justice, not just an individual choice. We trust and support the decision of the caller and take their lead in adopting the language they use in talking about their pregnancy and circumstances. </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">NYAAF does not scrutinize how many abortions someone has had, whether contraception was used, whether a sexual assault was reported, or any other information that is volunteered by our client, nor do we attempt to encourage anyone to behave in a certain way. If someone identifies a desire to learn about how to report an assault, how to avoid pregnancy, or other concerns, NYAAF provides information and makes appropriate referrals. </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">We consider these values to be at the core of our decision making as a board. They serve as an indispensable guide to all of our fundraising, programmatic, administrative, and communications decisions and actions. Our board, comprised of a diverse group of individuals who are all committed volunteers, all strongly believe in the values and mission of this organization. These shared values reflect an understanding of our work as part of a larger social justice and reproductive justice movement to ensure the full rights of all people. By building and supporting this movement, we strive for a world where everyone has the right and ability to determine their own future. </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'Arial'; font-weight: bold; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">Project Description: </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">The consultant/firm will be a dynamic facilitator with knowledge of the women’s, gender justice, and reproductive rights movements. They will have particularly deep knowledge of racial justice and organizational development, and will work within NYAAF’s Statement of Values. The consultant/firm should have at least five years of related experience; previous work with abortion funds preferred but not necessary. Experience with supporting radical organizational shifts would be especially welcomed. </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'ArialMT'; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">We intend to fill the position by mid June 2015, with the training and other deliverables due in late June/July. </span><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'Arial'; font-weight: bold; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">The total available budget for this project is between $5000- $10,000 approximately, plus travel costs. A competitive application will clearly demonstrate value relative to the proposed budget. </span></p> <p><span style="font-size: 12.000000pt; font-family: 'Arial'; font-weight: bold; color: rgb(12.156860%, 12.156860%, 12.156860%);">Broadly, responsibilities will include: </span></p> <ul style="list-style-type: disc;"> <li style="font-size: 12.000000pt; <strong>People are actually more likely to give money to a single individual, rather than an abstract group.</strong> Luckily, your donors aren’t only giving to an awesome cause. They’re also giving to an awesome individual: <strong>YOU</strong>. What’s a fun and easy way to remind them of this? Personalize your Bowl-a-Thon page! <em>(It’s sort of like decorating your locker in junior high school, if you went to a junior high school where your locker unleashed magical powers to fund abortion in New York.)</em></p> <p>Here are some starters to help inspire your page’s amazingness:</p> <p><U+25AA>  <strong>Step 1:</strong> Get some inspiration from NNAF’s “<a href="http://fundabortionnow.org/content/bowl-thon-page-week">Bowl-a-thon Page of the Week</a>” blog series (last week’s winner <a href="http://bowlathon.nnaf.org/participantpage.asp?userid=10122#.VRFcSPnF8jV">here</a>!) or by simply clicking around the <a href="http://bowlathon.nnaf.org/nnafbowl/default.asp">Bowl-a-thon homepage</a> and perusing the pages of other teams/bowlers.</p> <p><U+25AA>  <strong>Step 2:</strong> Find some imagery and text to use on your page! Some examples:</p> <p style="padding-left: 30px;"><U+25AA>  A <b>selfie</b> of you with a sign saying why you support abortion funds (check out Lauren Rankin’s “<a href="http://ifundabortions.tumblr.com/">I Fund Abortions</a>” Tumblr for examples!)<br /> <U+25AA>  A series of animated <b>GIFs</b> telling a compelling AND hilarious story (may we suggest <a href="http://giphy.com/">giphy.com</a> as a good GIF search engine?)<br /> <U+25AA>  Custom <b>memes</b> via <a href="http://memegenerator.net/">memegenerator.net</a><br /> <U+25AA>  Photos of <a href="http://www.nyaaf.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/bat14_-e1424980115918.jpg">you and your teammates</a> making funny faces<br /> <U+25AA>  <a href="http://fundabortionnow.org/news/bowl-thon-page-week-tana-richmond-reproductive-freedom-project">Personal stories</a> about why you support NYAAF, abortion funding, or bowling.</p> <p><U+25AA>  <strong>Step 3:</strong><b> Put it all together! </b>Simply <a href="http://bowlathon.blueskysweet.com/fundpage.asp?BranchID=97">log in to the Bowl-a-thon site</a>, select <em>Edit Your Page</em> from the menu on the left, and then select <em>Customize Your Page</em>. Here, you can post your memes and GIFs, manage your photos, change your fundraising goals, and edit your mission statement.</p> <p style="padding-left: 30px;"><U+25AA>  If you need more help, you can contact the NNAF <a href="mailto:techsupport@fundabortionnow.org">here</a>, or just send us an e-mail <a href="mailto:julia.bowl@nyaaf.org">directly</a>. We’re happy to answer your questions, provide inspiration, and help you make your page the best ever!</p> <p><U+25AA>  <strong>Step 4:</strong><b> Collaborate! </b>Once you have your page ready to go, you can do other cool stuff, like…..</p> <p style="padding-left: 30px;"><U+25AA>  <a href="http://fundabortionnow.org/content/bowl-thon-page-week">Nominate your page</a> (or your friend’s page, or your mom’s page) for NNAF’s Page of the Week<br /> <U+25AA>  Encourage others by commenting on the pages that you love<br /> <U+25AA>  Re-post your page on <a href="https://www.facebook.com/?_rdr">Facebook</a>, <a href="https://twitter.com/?lang=en">Twitter</a>, <a href="https://instagram.com/">Instagram</a>, the community board at your coffee shop, your dog’s forehead, etc.</p> <p><strong>Again, the most important thing is to make your page a reflection of YOU.</strong> Many of the pledges that you’ll receive will be from those who already know and love you, but who may not know why you care so deeply about this work. Let your page be your opportunity to tell them.</p> <p>As always, please reach out to us if you have any questions. Happy funding!</p> <p><b>Many thanks,</b><br /> Julia Jones and the NYAAF Team</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> 					</div><!-- .entry-content --> 		 		<footer class="entry-meta"> 									<span class="cat-links"> .entry-header -->  				<div class="entry-content"> 			<p>NYAAF Co-Chair, Alison Turkos provided testimony on NYAAF&#8217;s behalf in support of a resolution commemorative Roe. v. Wade and urging Congress to pass the <a href="https://www.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/senate-bill/1696">Women&#8217;s Health Projection Act</a>.</p> <p><a href="http://www.nyaaf.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/nyaaf.nycc_.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-1420" src="http://www.nyaaf.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/nyaaf.nycc_-1024x768.jpg" alt="nyaaf.nycc" width="584" height="438" /></a>Read NYAAF&#8217;s testimony below!</p> <p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><b>Testimony of the New York Abortion Access Fund<b> </b>before<b> </b>The New York City Council<b> </b>Committee on Women’s Issues<b> </b>regarding<b> </b>Proposed Resolution No. </b><a href="http://legistar.council.nyc.gov/LegislationDetail.aspx?ID=2123269&amp;GUID=CF254487-F95A-4830-8DEE-3FBEF0EDEC5C&amp;Options=&amp;Search="><b>T2015-2352</b></a></span></p> <p><b>January 21, 2015</b></p> <p><b>Thank you to the New York City Council for introducing this important resolution.</b></p> <p><b>My name is Alison Turkos and I am Co-Chair of the Board of Directors of the New York Abortion Access Fund, NYAAF. NYAAF supports anyone who is unable to pay fully for an abortion and is living in or travelling to New York State by providing financial assistance and connections to other resources. When an abortion clinic encounters a patient who needs financial assistance, NYAAF is one of the potential funding sources to which they turn. Over the last twelve years, we’ve pledged over $465,000 to help more than 1,500 people access abortion services. We are run completely by volunteers, are funded almost entirely by individual donations, and every single grant we make goes directly to an abortion clinic on behalf of a patient who cannot afford the cost. </b></p> <p><b>People come from all over the country to access abortion services in New York City, for a variety of reasons. NYAAF has helped people from as close as Pennsylvania and New Jersey to as far as California, Texas, Utah, and France. For some, abortion may be more affordable in NYC than in their home states, while others come because they may be able to have procedures done at later stages of pregnancy. Many people that NYAAF has helped found that New York has fewer barriers to accessing abortion care than surrounding states; some have even come from areas where there are no abortion providers at all.</b></p> <p><b>Many states have a limited number of providers, which leads to fewer clinics, which often means more travel time for many patients. Low-income individuals who seek funding from an abortion fund like NYAAF not only have a hard time coming up with funds for the abortion, but many times have to consider time off work, childcare, travel and hotel costs. </b><b>We work every day with clients who sell their belongings, go hungry for weeks as they save up their grocery money, or risk eviction by using their rent money to pay for an abortion.</b></p> <p><b>New York is lucky in that we don&#8217;t have some of the more onerous restrictions like those we&#8217;ve seen in recent years in Texas, which have succeeded in closing the doors of many of the state&#8217;s already over-burdened clinics. Ensuring that safe, legal abortion care is available to anyone who needs it requires institutional and political support for abortion providers. Clinics should be able to stay open without unnecessary structural requirements or other governmental hoops to jump through – hoops that serve not to protect patient health, but rather to delay access to safe legal services.</b><br /> <b>While we are privileged to live in a state where Medicaid covers abortion costs and where laws do not restrict access to abortion care, there are still many in New York who are unable to access abortion care. These are the many patients who turn to the New York Abortion Access Fund for funding. As the growing need for NYAAF’s funding shows, legalities can be meaningless when countless other barriers stand in your way. We are thankful for these courageous city council members who recognize that while abortion needs to be safe and legal, it also must be affordable </b><b><i>and</i></b><b> accessible. We hope this resolution will push people to think about abortion not just within the limiting framework of “choice”, but as a crucial component in the broader fight for economic and reproductive justice. Thank you.</b></p> 					</div><!-- .entry-content --> 		 		<footer class="entry-meta"> 												<span class="cat-links"> 				<span class="entry-utility-prep entry-utility-prep-cat-links">Posted in</span> <a href="http://www.nyaaf.org/category/uncategorized/" rel="category tag">Uncategorized</a>			</span> 									 									<span class="sep"> | </span> 						<span class="comments-link"><a href="http://www.nyaaf.org/2015/01/nyaaf-provides-testimony-before-the-new-york-city-council-committee-on-womens-issues/#respond"><span class="leave-reply">Leave a reply</span></a></span> 			 					</footer><!-- #entry-meta --> 	</article><!-- #post-1419 -->  				 						<nav id="nav-below"> 			<h3 class="assistive-text">Post navigation</h3> 			<div class="nav-previous"><a href="http://www.nyaaf.org/page/2/" ><span class="meta-nav">&larr;</span> Older posts</a></div> 			<div class="nav-next"></div> 		</nav><!-- #nav-above --> 	 			 			</div><!-- #content --> 		</div><!-- #primary -->  		<div id="secondary" class="widget-area" role="complementary"> 			<aside id="text-459584032" class="widget widget_text">			<div class="textwidget"><b>Do you need help paying for an abortion?
