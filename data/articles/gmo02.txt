time you looked closely at the list of strange, unidentifiable ingredients on your package of food? Unless the package is labeled with a USDA Organic or a Non-GMO Project certified insignia, processed foods typically contain a plethora of GMOs—typically in some form of processed corn (have you watched the movie <a href="http://gmo-awareness.com/resources/movies-to-watch-gmo-and-more/" target="_blank">King Corn</a> yet?). Below is a list of &#8220;invisible&#8221; GMO ingredients (Source: the <a href="http://www.nongmoshoppingguide.com" target="_blank">Non-GMO Shopping Guide</a>):</p> <p>Aspartame (<i>also called AminoSweet®, NutraSweet®, Equal Spoonful®, Canderel®,  BeneVia®, E951</i>)<br /> Baking powder<br /> Canola oil (rapeseed oil)<br /> Caramel color<br /> Cellulose<br /> Citric acid<br /> Cobalamin (Vitamin B12)<br /> Colorose<br /> Condensed milk<br /> Confectioners sugar<br /> Corn flour<br /> Corn masa<br /> Corn meal<br /> Corn oil<br /> Corn sugar<br /> Corn syrup<br /> Cornstarch<br /> Cottonseed oil<br /> Cyclodextrin<br /> Cystein<br /> Dextrin<br /> Dextrose<br /> Diacetyl<br /> Diglyceride<br /> Erythritol<br /> Equal<br /> Food starch<br /> Fructose (any form)<br /> Glucose<br /> Glutamate<br /> Glutamic acid<br /> Glycerides<br /> Glycerin<br /> Glycerol<br /> Glycerol monooleate<br /> Glycine<br /> Hemicellulose<br /> High fructose corn syrup (HFCS)<br /> Hydrogenated starch<br /> Hydrolyzed vegetable protein<br /> Inositol<br /> Inverse syrup<br /> Inversol<br /> Invert sugar<br /> Isoflavones<br /> Lactic acid<br /> Lecithin<br /> Leucinelysine<br /> Malitol<br /> Malt<br /> Malt syrup<br /> Malt extract<br /> Maltodextrin<br /> Maltose<br /> Mannitol<br /> Methylcellulose<br /> Milk powder<br /> Milo starch<br /> Modified food starch<br /> Modified starch<br /> Mono- and diglycerides<br /> Monosodium Glutamate (MSG)<br /> Nutrasweet<br /> Oleic acid<br /> Phenylalanine<br /> Phytic acid<br /> Protein isolate<br /> Shoyu<br /> Sorbitol<br /> Soy flour<br /> Soy isolates<br /> Soy lecithin<br /> Soy milk<br /> Soy oil<br /> Soy protein<br /> Soy protein isolate<br /> Soy sauce<br /> Starch<br /> Stearic acid<br /> Sugar (unless specified as cane sugar)<br /> Tamari<br /> Tempeh<br /> Teriyaki marinades<br /> Textured vegetable protein<br /> Threonine<br /> Tocopherols (vitamin E)<br /> Tofu<br /> Trehalose<br /> Triglyceride<br /> Vegetable fat<br /> Vegetable oil<br /> Vitamin B12<br /> Vitamin C (see note*)<br /> Vitamin E<br /> Whey<br /> Whey powder<br /> Xanthan gum</p> <p><i>*According to the Non-GMO Shopping Guide, Vitamin C (ascorbic acid), although usually derived from corn, is &#8220;probably not&#8221; GM because it is not likely made in North America. However other sources like <a href="http://www.gmo-compass.org/eng/database/e-numbers/202.vitamin_c.html" target="_blank">GMO Compass</a> and <a href="http://www.organicconsumers.org/Organic/vitccontro.cfm" target="_blank">Organic Consumers Association</a> believe that Vitamin C is almost always GM, because of the newer GM-based production technology which is widely used in China, considering that China is the primary source of Vitamin C.</i></p> <p>&#8212;<br /> © GMO-Awareness.com, 2011.
