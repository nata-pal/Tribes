'outbound-article', 'http://i0.wp.com/static.bangordailynews.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/272/files/2015/08/news-organic-farm-angelina-alpaca_080615.jpg', '']);"  target="_blank" rel="nofollow"></a>A national survey to learn about the research needs of today’s organic farmers, the Organic Farming Research Foundation last month began emailing out the 2015 National Survey of Organic Farmers.</p> <p>The survey, according to OFRF, invites “all certified organic farmers in the U.S. to share their experiences, and let the science community know what areas of research are most needed to advance organic farming.”</p> <p>The Maine Organic Farmers and Gardeners Association reported on July 31, “The 2015 National Survey of Organic Farmers will be emailed to every certified organic farmer with a listed email address on the U.S. Department of Agriculture’s (USDA) certification database, which lists a total of 13,352 certified organic farmers. Farmers without a listed email address will receive a mailed postcard asking them to access the survey via OFRF’s website at www.ofrf.org. Organic certification organizations nationwide have also agreed to help distribute the survey to their members.”</p> <p>The confidential survey, according to MOFGA, “asks for data on farm size, production and location, as well as collecting detailed information about organic farming challenges and farmers’ most pressing information needs, such as pest control, soil health, water conservation strategies, pesticide drift and GMO contamination of organic crops.”</p> <p>Survey results will be used to update OFRF’s National Organic Research Agenda, a “roadmap for the USDA and other research institutions, identifying the issues most critical to the success of organic farmers,” OFRF reported. For details, visit www.ofrf.org.</p> <p>In other organic ag news, the Common Ground Country Fair poster design contest is underway. Each year MOFGA hosts this contest to find a design “that captures the essence of the Common Ground Country Fair.” The winning design is featured annually on the Common Ground Country Fair poster and T-shirt. MOFGA is accepting entries for the 2016 poster until Wednesday, Aug. 12 at noon. For details on how to submit, visit http://www.mofga.org/TheFair/Poster/tabid/107/Default.aspx.</p> <p>MOFGA also issued a list of upcoming farm apprenticeship program workshops. They include:</p> <p>Aug. 10 — <strong>Cut Flower Primer</strong>. Mon., 5 p.m., Broadturn Farm, Scarborough.</p> <p>Aug. 12 — <strong>Farming with Carnivores</strong>. Weds., 5 p.m., Two Coves Farm, Harpswell.</p> <p>Aug. 19 — <strong>Next Steps: From Apprentice to Farmer</strong>. Weds., 5 p.m., Tender Soles Farm, Richmond.</p> <p>Aug. 25 — <strong>Preserving the Harvest</strong>: Hands-On Food Preservation. Tues., UMaine Cooperative Extension, Lisbon Falls. $10 fee.</p> <p>Aug. 26 — <strong>Johnny’s Selected Seeds Tour</strong>. Weds., 5 p.m., Johnny’s Research Farm, Albion.</p> <p>For more information, visit <a href="http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.mofga.org%2FPrograms%2FFarmApprenticeships%2Ftabid%2F502%2FDefault.aspxsa=Dsntz=1usg=AFQjCNHBc1If4BogoAPXgObaKTI3VjWSdw" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.mofga.org%2FPrograms%2FFarmApprenticeships%2Ftabid%2F502%2FDefault.aspxsa=Dsntz=1usg=AFQjCNHBc1If4BogoAPXgObaKTI3VjWSdw', 'http://www.mofga.org/Programs/FarmApprenticeships/tabid/502/Default.aspx']);" class="c7"  target="_blank" rel="nofollow">http://www.mofga.org/Programs/FarmApprenticeships/tabid/502/Default.aspx</a>.</p> <p>Other upcoming events include:</p> <p>Aug. 8 — Free Gardening Workshop for Children and Their Families. Regional School Unit (RSU) 1 L.O.C.A.L.
