The Gay Vegans: Crappy vegan food is bad for veganism </h3> <div class='post-header'> <div class='post-header-line-1'></div> </div> <div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3230679214634627040' itemprop='description articleBody'> <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"> <a &quot;Trebuchet MS&quot;,sans-serif;">By Dan Hanley</span></span></b><br /> <br /> <span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: &quot;Trebuchet MS&quot;,sans-serif;">I have always loved this line. I heard it when in a session at Vida Vegan Con II in Portland, Oregon two years ago. I was in a session to learn about writing restaurant reviews given by Grant Butler, a columnist with The Oregonian.<br /> <br /> It has stayed with me ever since and I consider it every time I write a restaurant review.<br /> <br /> I have thought of it a lot since moving to the Los Angeles area while we experience so many new vegan opportunities. Having been to LA many times, we've known that this is a vegan food mecca. Living here is different, as new experiences pop up almost every day.<br /> <br /> There have been some eating experiences that have blown us away, where we find us like at each other going "oh my goodness!".<br /> <br /> And then there have been experiences that were not so mind blowing.  <br /> <br /> On the top of my list was a dessert Mike and I saw. We both instantly drooled. Anticipation was very high as the server brought it to our table. Then utter disappointment. It was dry and not very tasty.<br /> <br /> We have had several moments like this. We read reviews, we get excited, we drive way out of our way to try a restaurant and then we are presented with food that is average, and in some cases not good on any level.<br /> <br /> How can this change? I'm starting with this post. At the restaurant where the dessert was a huge disappointment, I took the server aside and gave very honest feedback. I wouldn't do a restaurant review based on just one experience.<br /> <br /> I think it is vastly important that we let restaurants know when their food is sub-par. Of course food is very personal, but if you get something that is totally off base, let the folks know.<br /> <br /> I think about someone who wants to try a vegan meal and gets crappy food, and how that affects not only veganism but the animals as well.</span></span><br /> <br /> <span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: &quot;Trebuchet MS&quot;,sans-serif;"><i>This post originally appeared on Dan Hanley's website <a href="http://thegayvegans.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">The Gay Vegans</a>.
