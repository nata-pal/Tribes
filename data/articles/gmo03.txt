.entry-header -->  	<div class="entry-content"> 		<p><img class="size-full wp-image-380 aligncenter" title="GMO Tomato" src="http://gmoawareness.files.wordpress.com/2011/04/gmo-tomato.jpg?w=584" alt=""   /></p> <h1><em>GMO stands for Genetically Modified Organism. Other names for the process include Genetic Engineering (GE) or Genetic Modification (GM), which are one and the same.</em></h1> <h1>How is a GMO Different from Hybridization / Cross-Breeding?</h1> <p>Genetic modification is the process of forcing genes from one species into another <em>entirely unrelated</em> species. Unlike cross breeding or hybridization—both of which involve two <em>related</em> species and have been done without ill effects for centuries—genetic engineering forcefully breaches the naturally-occuring barriers between species.</p> <p><strong>Example:</strong>  Corn + DNA from soil bacteria that is naturally immune to RoundUp herbicide + e.coli bacteria + soil bacteria that causes tumors in plants (which enables the plant&#8217;s cell wall to be breached) = RoundUp Ready Corn (one of several RoundUp Ready crops engineered by Monsanto).</p> <p>Other examples of GMOs include strawberries and tomatoes injected with fish genes to protect the fruit from freezing, goats <a href="http://abcnews.go.com/Technology/CuttingEdge/story?id=98095&amp;page=1" target="_blank">injected with spider genes</a> to produce milk with proteins stronger than kevlar for use in industrial products, salmon that are genetically engineered with a <a href="http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2010/09/100925105209.htm" target="_blank">growth hormone</a> that allow them to keep growing larger, dairy cows injected with the genetically engineered hormone rBGH (also known as rBST) to increase milk production, and <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/03/01/AR2007030101495.html" target="_blank">rice injected with human genes</a> to produce pharmaceuticals.</p> <p><img class="size-full wp-image-382 alignnone" style="margin-right:15px;" title="GMO Cows milk_pro" src="http://gmoawareness.files.wordpress.com/2011/04/gmo-cows-milk_pro.jpg?w=584" alt=""   /></p> <h1>How are Crops Genetically Modified?</h1> <p>Foreign DNA is inserted into the primary plant species using one of three methods:</p> <ol> <li>E.coli bacteria is combined with a soil bacteria that causes tumors that allows the foreign bacteria to breach the host plant’s cells.</li> <li>Electricity is applied to the host plant to rupture its cell walls, thus allowing the foreign DNA to invade; or</li> <li>A “gene gun” blasts the engineered DNA directly into the plant’s cells.</li> </ol> <p>For a more visual explanation, watch <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jAP6ZtfP9ZQ" target="_blank">this video</a> (the process graphics begin at the 2.20 minute mark).</p> <p>Because the injected genes can come from bacteria, viruses, insects, animals or even <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/03/01/AR2007030101495.html" target="_blank">humans</a>, GMOs are also known as &#8220;transgenic&#8221; organisms. But this too is another misleading statement. What humans have been &#8220;doing for centuries&#8221; is called hybridization. <em><strong>Hybridization is NOT genetic engineering.</strong></em></p> <p>Genetic engineering involves the combination of two entirely unrelated species in a lab—species that could never cross or coexist in nature and thus require complex techniques to forcefully combine the genes.</p> <p>The second most common argument in favor of GMOs is the biotech industry&#8217;s claims that the FDA has &#8220;thoroughly evaluated&#8221; GM foods and found them safe. focusing their current marketing efforts on <a href="http://www.twnside.org.sg/title/service93.htm" target="_blank">third world countries</a> with the promise of feeding their hungry.</p> <p>•  By labeling any public outcry — whether scientist or consumer — as misinformed, progress-phobic, anti-science propaganda.</p> <h2>How Do You Know Who to Believe?</h2> <p>If you&#8217;re like us, you&#8217;re feeling pretty overwhelmed at this point. The more you read, the more you&#8217;re wondering how something like this could have gotten so far without your knowledge.</p> <p>Please don&#8217;t take our word for it. Do your own research. Yes, you&#8217;ll find some muddy waters when you do. Wherever possible, we encourage you to do what we did: ask these three key questions (courtesy of the <a href="http://labelgmos.org/" target="_blank">Label GMOs</a> website):</p> <ul> <ul> <li>Who is conducting the research and what is their relationship with the biotech industry?</li> <li>Who is funding the research?  Who is their parent funder?  And theirs? And theirs?</li> <li>Who monetarily benefits from the results of the research?</li> </ul> </ul> <p><a href="http://gmoawareness.files.wordpress.com/2011/04/monsanto-fda.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-853" title="Monsanto FDA" src="http://gmoawareness.files.wordpress.com/2011/04/monsanto-fda.png?w=584" alt=""   /></a></p> <p>Last but not least, when you read information from the FDA and USDA, take the time to investigate the names behind the statements, and do a quick Wikipedia search on those individuals. You may (or may not) be surprised to learn how many of the people currently employed by the FDA were formerly employed by key corporations responsible for genetic engineering before they were &#8220;assigned&#8221; to work for the FDA.</p> <p>Since this technology is comparatively new, and there is still no data on the long term effects of human consumption of GMOs, are you comfortable being part of an experiment?</p> <p>Sadly, most US consumers have no idea about any of this.</p> <p>It&#8217;s time to wake up and decide if the <a href="http://www.aaemonline.org/gmopost.html" target="_blank">impact of GMO foods</a> &#8212; on you, your family, and the environment &#8212; <a href="http://gmoawareness.wordpress.com/gmo-risks/" target="_blank">is a risk</a> you&#8217;re willing to take.</p> <h2>Where Can You Learn More?</h2> <p>To view (or download if you wish) a PDF document containing an in-depth, evidence-based examination of the &#8220;Myths and Truths&#8221; about GMOs written by a noted geneticist, <a href="http://xgmo.files.wordpress.com/2012/09/gmo_myths_and_truths_1-31.pdf" target="_blank">click here.</a></p> <p>To keep abreast of breaking news, information and helpful recommendations about avoiding GMOs, consider &#8220;liking&#8221; our <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/GMO-Awareness/116246468464192" target="_blank">GMO Awareness Facebook page</a>.</p> <p>&#8212;<br /> © GMO-Awareness.com 2011–2014.
