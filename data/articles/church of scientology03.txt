According to the American Religious Identification Survey, <a href="http://blogs.villagevoice.com/runninscared/2011/07/scientologists_1.php">only 25,0000</a> Americans consider themselves Scientologists.</p>  <p>That said, interest in the religion is huge. Last week, HBO released a documentary on the Church of Scientology (COS), called <em>Going Clear, </em><a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/03/31/going-clear-ratings_n_6979596.html">which was watched by 1.7 million people</a> (not counting those who streamed online), more than any HBO documentary in nearly a decade.</p>  <p>The documentary addresses Scientology’s tax-exempt status, which it won from the IRS in 1993. The film alleges that the COS was only declared tax-exempt by the IRS after it waged a decades-long campaign against the agency, which included filing dozens of lawsuits against both the organization and individual IRS workers, and hiring private investors and fake journalists to dig up incriminating information about individual IRS workers.</p>  <p>When the IRS finally granted the the church tax-exempt status, after an unconventional meeting between the then-commissioner Fred T.
