			</div> 		   		<p>CENTENNIAL, Colo. &#8212; A verdict has been reached in the James Holmes Colorado theater shooting trial. The verdict will be read at 7 p.m. EST.</p> <p>Jurors reviewed a graphic crime scene video before deciding whether to give James Holmes the death penalty. The panel of nine women and three men asked for the 45-minute recording Friday morning.</p> <p>Jurors are making their decision after a 15-week trial in which they saw 2,695 pieces of evidence and heard from 306 witnesses. Relatives of those killed in the massacre were the last to take the stand, offering heartbreaking stories of loss.</p> <p>Defense attorneys objected that the gruesome images taken immediately after the massacre would be prejudicial. But Judge Carlos Samour, Jr. allowed it and said jurors would only have 50 minutes to watch the tape. He warned them not to let it prejudice their deliberations.</p> <p>Last month the same jury rejected Holmes&#8217; insanity defense and convicted him of 165 counts of murder, attempted murder and weapons charges.</p> <p>Now jurors must decide whether Holmes should be executed for killing 12 during the 2012 assault, which also injured 70. Jurors appear to be nearing the end of Phase 3 of his sentencing, which could include the death penalty.</p> <p>CBS Denver Legal Analyst Karen Steinhauser, a Denver defense attorney and former prosecutor, told CBS Denver the request may indicate the jury hasn&#8217;t made up their mind.</p> <p>&#8220;I think it means that the jury is conflicted in terms of this ultimate decision and I think it means that there is at least one person who is not convinced beyond a reasonable doubt that death is the appropriate sentence and other jurors may be saying, &#8216;You need to look at this again,'&#8221; said Steinhauser.</p> 		<div id="geo-post-255807" class="geo geo-post" style="display: none"> 			<span class="latitude">39.580745</span> 			<span class="longitude">-104.877173</span>
