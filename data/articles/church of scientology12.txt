class="entry-title">Proposal to Mike Godwin: Creating a &#8220;Church of Scientology Exception&#8221; to Godwin&#8217;s&nbsp;Law</h1> 			</header><!-- .entry-header -->  												</a> 								<div class="entry-content"> 					<p>A proposal submitted to Mike Godwin for his consideration: Make a &#8220;Church of Scientology&#8221; exception to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mike_Godwin#cite_note-20">Godwin&#8217;s Law</a>.<strong><br /> </strong></p> <p>Why? I argue that it is not incorrect to compare the nature, structure, and operations of the Church of Scientology to the Nazis. As I will show in this essay, the Church of Scientology is a multi-billion dollar transnational Master Race group which has a <a href="http://scientologymoneyproject.com/2015/02/02/the-horriying-real-secret-of-scientology/">call for genocide</a> embedded in the writings of L. Ron Hubbard.</p> <p>Secretly operated and managed by a <a href="http://scientologymoneyproject.com/2015/06/06/if-the-sea-org-doesnt-legally-exist-how-does-it-run-scientology/">legally non-existent paramilitary organization</a> called the Sea Org, the Church of Scientology finances and operates a psycho-terrorism, propaganda, and intelligence gathering agency called the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Office_of_Special_Affairs">Office of Special Affairs</a>. While the Church presently lacks the means to conduct a genocide, its decades-long malicious conduct of &#8220;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientology_controversies#Fair_Game">Fair Game</a>&#8221; argues that it would do so if it had the means.</p> <div style="width: 669px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img src="http://www.xenu.net/archive/photoalbum/7a-1200.jpg" alt="" width="659" height="407" /><p class="wp-caption-text">The Church of Scientology&#8217;s fanatical paramilitary is called the Sea Org.</p></div> <p>My request in no way trivializes the Holocaust. Indeed, my proposal for an exception to Godwin&#8217;s Law is based upon the following very serious and frightening facts concerning the Church of Scientology:</p> <p>1. L. Ron Hubbard declared the Church of Scientology to be <a href="http://scientologymoneyproject.com/2015/02/02/the-horriying-real-secret-of-scientology/">a Master Race group</a> called <em><strong>Homo Novis</strong></em>:</p> <blockquote><p>[Re: “Homo Scientologicus”] : “Man had to cease to be Homo Sapiens and had to become Homo Scientologicus in order to accomplish any action that was anywhere near efficient in South Africa.” — L. Ron Hubbard, PAB 119 1 September 1957 The Big Auditing Problem</p></blockquote> <p>Hubbard elaborated upon his notion of Scientologists as a master race in 1965:</p> <blockquote><p>“HOMO NOVIS, 1. Homo man, novis, new. (BCR, p. 12) 2. a theta-animated mest body possessed of new and desirable attributes; a mest clear, a good, sane rational mest being about a skyscraper higher than Homo sapiens. CSI, RTC, the Flag Service Org (which employs over 900 Sea Org members), the Saint Hill Churches, in short, all high ranking organizations are Sea Org Churches. Being a ‘Sea Org Church’ means that the church’s function is important enough to Scientology to warrant the attention of a significant number of Sea Org members.</p> <p>“Sea Org rank nominally carries with it no ecclesiastical authority in the sense that Sea Org members still take orders from the ecclesiastical leaders of whichever Scientology organization they join. Upon closer analysis, however, this appears to be a distinction without a difference because in a Sea Org church the ecclesiastical authority necessarily resides in a Sea Org member.”</p></blockquote> <p>As mentioned in our previous essay, the Church of Scientology is composed of legally separate religious corporations. Ostensibly, each corporation is independent and responsible for its own affairs. This is stated in contracts Scientologists sign to receive services from these legally separate churches:</p> <p style="text-align:center;"> <img class="aligncenter" src="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/Contract.jpg" alt="" width="716" height="112" /></p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <p>The <strong><i>deceptis visus</i></strong> cited by the US Claims Court in 1992 thus becomes apparent: In order to achieve a unity of command over the legally separate and independent Scientology religious corporations and their employees, the Sea Org exists as the secret governing body of the Church of Scientology. Therefore, whoever commands the Sea Org commands the Church of Scientology. This person, at present, is Captain David Miscavige.</p> <p>The Sea Org is like the Mafia: It exists and it operates and controls the Church of Scientology, However, Scientology and its lawyers have made it virtually impossible to prove that Sea Org members derive any power whatsoever from their ceremonial ranks in a symbolic and legally nonexistent religious order. Again, to reiterate what we discussed in our last essay, David Miscavige’s attorney Wallace Jefferson declared in 2014 in Rathbun v. Miscavige et. al.:</p> <blockquote><p>Plaintiff asserts that Mr. Miscavige exercised control because he leads the Sea Organization, a religious order within Scientology. But the “Sea Org” is not a corporate entity; it has no physical or legal existence. It is not incorporated or established pursuant to legal formalities. It has no constitution, charter or bylaws, and no formal or informal ecclesiastical, corporate, or other management structure. It has no directors, officers, managing agents, or other executives; no employees, staff members, or volunteers; no income; no disbursements, no bank accounts or other assets; no liabilities; no stationery; no office, home, address, or telephone number. It does not create or maintain any financial, personnel, or other records. It can neither give nor receive orders because it has no one to either give or receive them or to carry them out. It cannot sue or be sued. The evidence Mrs. Rathbun has submitted fails to establish a prima facie basis for an alter ego finding, because none of it involves the defendants’ purported contacts relating to this suit, nor does any of it speak to the organizations’ current practices.</p></blockquote> <p>— Per David Miscavige and his attorneys, there is no such organization as the Sea Organization.<br /> — Per David Miscavige’s attorney Wallace Jefferson, the Sea Org cannot have any members or volunteers.<br /> — There can be no “religious commitment” to the Sea Org because the Sea Org does not exist to be committed to.<br /> — The Sea Org cannot have volunteers as the Sea Org does not exist to volunteer for.<br /> — The Sea Org has no address and does not exist anywhere. Neither you or I nor anyone else can mail a letter to the Sea Org.<br /> — Sea Org members sign a “religious commitment” that is craftily worded so as to say nothing about the Sea Org being an actual organization:</p> <p style="text-align:center;"><img class="aligncenter" src="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/SeaOrgContract.jpg" alt="" width="480" height="808" /></p> <p style="text-align:left;">The real details of the Sea Org are found in the staff contract Sea Org members sign:</p> <p style="text-align:center;"><img class="aligncenter" src="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/SeaOrgContract2.jpg" alt="" /></p> <p>The Sea Org controls the Church of Scientology and yet there is no such organization as the Sea Organization. If this seems contradictory and confusing it is because it was designed to be so. The only way any of this legal tissue of lies begins to make any possible sense is when considered in terms of L. Ron Hubbard’s words about the design and construction of the Church of Scientology:<br /> “If anybody tried to attack a Scientology organization and pick it up and move it out of the perimeter or go over the hills with it today — this happened to us once — why, they would find themselves involved in the most confounded weird mass of legal — well, it is just like quicksand. Quicksand. It’s an interesting trick. Every time they shoot at you on the right side of a horse, you’re on the left side of the horse; and then they prove conclusively you’re on the left side of the horse, you prove conclusively that you’re on the right side of the horse. They go mad after a while. This is what the basic legal structure is.”</p> <p>Marty Rathbun’s affidavit in Miscavige vs. Miscavige et. al. is very instructive in this regard. archival purposes)</p> <p>The Church of Scientology is an opposite world where black is white and up is down. One can be certain that the Church is doing the exact opposite of whatever it is publicly promoting.</p> <p>One need only read the latest online edition of Scientology’s propaganda organ <i>Freedom</i> magazine to see the hypocrisy. We’ve taken some recent <i>Freedom</i> headlines and contrast them with the facts.</p> <p><a href="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Freedom1a.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22603" src="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Freedom1a.jpg" alt="Freedom1a" width="640" height="129" /></a></p> <p>There is no “Freedom of Information” in the Church of Scientology whatsoever:<br /> • The Church routinely makes Scientologists sign bonds calling for penalties of $25,000 for violating confidentiality.<br /> • Scientology will not publish truthful membership numbers and still insists it has millions of members<br /> • Scientologists are made to sign a contract stating that they do not own their own pc folders and are not allowed to read their pc folders.<br /> • Knowledge Reports – snitching on others – is a way of life inside Scientology.<br /> • The whereabouts of Shelly Miscavige is a heavily-guarded Church secret<br /> • The Church does not open its books for financial inspection.<br /> • The Church does not account for how it spends parishioner donations.<br /> <a href="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Freedom2a.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22604" src="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Freedom2a.jpg" alt="Freedom2a" width="641" height="117" /></a></p> <p>The Church of Scientology has spent decades making “up” into “down.” Examples of Scientology propaganda:<br /> • David Miscavige is portrayed as a “global ecclesiastical leader” when he has never actually done any of the things real global ecclesiastical leaders do. For example, Miscavige has never been invited to address the United Nations; has never addressed the US Congress or any other elected political body; has never been photographed with any global political or religious leaders; has never funded and opened an orphanage, a hospital, or a university. Miscavige has appeared only on television twice — in 1992 on ABC’s <i>Nightline</i> with Ted Koppel and in a 1998 A&amp;E interview.<br /> • Scientology tells the public it is a religion, but tells its members it’s an exact science with the only explanations for the nature and origin of the universe.<br /> • L. Ron Hubbard claimed <i>Dianetics</i> was “a milestone for Man comparable to his discovery of fire and superior to his inventions of the wheel and the arch.”<br /> • Scientology Orgs are mostly empty and yet Scientology insists there is 47x expansion going on<br /> • The Church still claims the Guardian’s Office was disbanded when the GO was simply morphed into Office of Special Affairs. Led by former GO Snow White Programs Op Linda Hamel, OSA is tasked with churning out a steady stream of Scientology propaganda and crafting a never-ending series of hateful websites against former Scientologists and critics. And like the GO, OSA has an unlimited budget to spy on former Church members, critics, and on persons of interest to David Miscavige.<br /> • The Church of Scientology claims to be “Humanitarian” when Scientologists secretly deride humans as “homo saps” and “wogs.” Scientologists believe themselves to be Homo Novis, a master race superior in every way to humans.<br /> <a href="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Freedom3a.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22605" src="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Freedom3a.jpg" alt="Freedom3a" width="640" height="113" /></a></p> <p>Fighting to know the facts in the Church of Scientology is a crime:<br /> • Scientologists are not allowed to know what their donations are spent on.<br /> • Scientologists are not allowed to know why certain people have suddenly disappeared<br /> • Scientologists can be severely punished for reading “critical websites” on the internet.<br /> • New Scientologists are not allowed to know the contents of the OT Levels.<br /> • Scientologists are not allowed to know the contents of their pc folders or Ethics folders.<br /> • Scientologists are not allowed to talk to family or friends who have been declared “suppressive.”<br /> • Scientologists are not allowed to know the real details of L. Ron Hubbard’s death.<br /> • Scientologists are not allowed to know the real details of how David Miscavige took over the Church.<br /> • Scientologists are strictly forbidden to talk about their case outside of session. No Scientologist is allowed to know how any other Scientologist is actually doing in session. Even husbands and wives are forbidden to talk to each other about their auditing.<br /> <a href="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Freedom4a.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22606" src="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Freedom4a.jpg" alt="Freedom4a" width="640" height="200" /></a></p> <p>This headline is yet another exercise in Scientology hypocrisy:<br /> • The Church of Scientology uses child labor and works children 80 to 100 hours per week or more.<br /> • The Rehabilitation Project Force is a prison-like detention center whose inmates have no legal rights and are not allowed to speak to anyone except other prisoners.<br /> • Sea Org members are treated as prisoners, live in guarded dormitories, and are guarded during their working hours to prevent their escape.<br /> • The Church of Scientology engages in human trafficking. For example, the Scientology Sea Org recruits heavily in Eastern Europe for its labor force at Flag. Once overseas workers arrive at Flag, their passports are confiscated and they are heavily guarded as they are moved by bus from berthing to Flag. Aside from subsistence room and board, these workers are paid as little as possible, typically about forty cents per hour for working 100+ hour weeks.<br /> • The Church of Scientology makes its members sign its infamous “kidnap contract” allowing the Church to kidnap and hold them against their will if they are deemed Type III, a term referring to a psychotic break. This is the same type of contract Scientology agents used to pull <a href="http://blogs.villagevoice.com/runninscared/2011/09/lisa_mcpherson_top_25_crippling_scientology.php" target="_blank">Lisa McPherson</a> out of psychiatric hospitalization following her psychotic break on a Clearwater street in which she stripped her clothes off and asked passersby for help.<br /> • The Church of Scientology operates “recovery teams” to hunt down and capture escaped Sea Org members.<br /> <a href="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Freedom5a.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22607" src="http://tonyortega.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Freedom5a.jpg" alt="Freedom5a" width="640" height="153" /></a></p> <p>This headline is one of those “Scientology is lying again” moments. There is no privacy for anyone in the Church of Scientology:<br /> • All auditing sessions are videotaped.<br /> • All sec checks are videotaped.<br /> • Sea Org berthing is subject to unannounced searches.<br /> • David Miscavige and the Office of Special Affairs use tax-exempt dollars to spy on people, purchase phone records, and dig through trash.<br /> • Pat Broeker was secretly spied on for twenty-five years at a cost of $12 million.<br /> • David Miscavige <a href="http://tonyortega.org/2015/04/20/hear-the-full-police-interview-of-scientology-spy-daniel-powell-about-stalking-ron-miscavige-sr/" target="_blank">spied on his own father</a> for at least eighteen months at a cost of $10,000 per week (and probably a lot more).<br /> • OSA rented a house to spy on the Marty and Monique Rathbun for three years, using hidden cameras.<br /> • OSA’s main job is to continually gather information on Church members and Church critics alike.<br /> • The Church of Scientology admitted to engaging in burglary and wiretapping in Operation Snow White.</p> Scientology presents a copyrighted problem for which it offers the only possible and copyrighted solution.</p> <p>Based upon this copyrighted content, it is quite easy to see that Scientology can exist completely apart from any tax exempt status. Scientology is most fundamentally a business and, as such, must rely heavily upon contracts to transact business and to enforce the confidentiality of both its overt and covert operations.</p> <hr /> <p style="text-align:center;"><em><strong>There is no faith in Scientology; there is only a series of business contracts.</strong> </em></p> <hr /> <p>The Church of Scientology long ago legally argued that its Xenu-based cosmology is a trade secret. One suddenly realizes they are continually being spied on by both seen and unseen agents of the despot. One is asked to come in for interviews.</p> <p>When traveling in a despotic foreign land one has none of the civil and legal rights they enjoy back home. There are ever-presents risks of losing all one&#8217;s money and sanity in this foreign land. </p> <p>If you escape Scientologyland and then seek to warn others of the dangers therein, Scientologists will seek to attack you overtly and covertly. </p> <p>Scientology is a small and dangerous place.</p> <hr /> <p>The monies paid and the many contracts Scientologists must sign argue for the commercial nature of the Scientology enterprise.</p> <p>When the Church of Scientology loses its tax exemption it will be reconfigured into a slimmed-down secular contract-based business offering a particular product and method, i.e. a copyrighted fusion of Freudian psychotherapy, reincarnation, and claimed esoteric-cosmological phenomena investigatable only by e-meter.</p> <hr /> <p>Scientology uses its tax exemption to cruelly exploit its captive labor force; engage in deceptive fundraising practices; hoard and accumulate tax free wealth; attack its critics and former members; pay for an army of lawyers, influence, and friends in high place; and be shielded by First Amendment protections while pursuing while doing these things.
