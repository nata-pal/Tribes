And most people don&#8217;t realise that if you&#8217;re sharing any of that data with your friends then apps used by those friends can see that data too!</p> <p>Advertisers, Facebook app developers, and Facebook ad tech partners don&#8217;t get a direct look at your personal data. They won&#8217;t see that my name is Jim Edwards, my phone number is 07xxxx, I&#8217;m male, and I work at Business Insider — Facebook hashes and anonymises all the data to protect user&#8217;s privacy and gives it back to partners in bulk so they can&#8217;t identify individuals — but nevertheless, this data is being used in order to serve you better-targeted ads.</p> <p><strong>If you&#8217;re worried about your privacy on Facebook, you can do two things:</strong></p> <ol> <li>Opt out of ad tracking</li> <li>Look up the list of app companies that are logged in to your Facebook account, and edit that list.</li> </ol> <p>The second step — which shows you just how much info you&#8217;re giving away to companies that you&#8217;ve probably forgotten about — can be rather alarming if you haven&#8217;t done it in a while</p> <h3>We&#8217;ll deal with the ad-tracking first, as that is easiest.</h3> <p>You can comfort yourself a little bit with the knowledge that the ads being targeted at you are coming anonymously and in bulk, at everyone who is in some way similar to you.
