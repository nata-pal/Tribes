bad thing. <a href="http://spectrum.ieee.org/automaton/robotics/artificial-intelligence/we-should-not-ban-killer-robots">I don&rsquo;t think we should ban killer robots</a>, but <a href="http://spectrum.ieee.org/automaton/robotics/artificial-intelligence/why-we-really-should-ban-autonomous-weapons">lots of people think that it&rsquo;s very clear that we should</a>. There&rsquo;s been an enormous debate in the comments of these articles, and on Twitter as well. Georgia Tech&rsquo;s&nbsp;<a href="http://spectrum.ieee.org/automaton/robotics/artificial-intelligence/autonomous-robotic-weapons-could-reduce-civilian-casualties">Ron Arkin weighed in this week</a>,&nbsp;and we&rsquo;ll have another expert perspective next week. We&rsquo;re not expecting to reach a consensus here: there&rsquo;s no easy (or even unambiguously correct) answer. What we&rsquo;re trying to do, though, is to provide as many perspectives on the issue as possible to help you inform your own thinking. The ethics of robotics is something that we&rsquo;re very interested in, and we&rsquo;ll be returning to it in a variety of contexts over this year and next.</p>  <p>Still, that&rsquo;s all some heavy, heavy stuff, you know?
