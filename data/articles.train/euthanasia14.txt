class="colour-black" href="/2015/08/">7 August 2015</a> </time> <h1>Healthy woman kills herself because “growing old is awful”.</h1> <div class="clearfix"> <div class="author clearfix"> <img alt='' src='http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/7e885e6cd376aaf86d6d5673e29beb13?s=50&#038;d=mm&#038;r=pg' srcset='http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/7e885e6cd376aaf86d6d5673e29beb13?s=100&amp;d=mm&amp;r=pg 2x' class='avatar avatar-50 photo' height='50' width='50'> <h2><a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/author/emily-purcell/" rel="author">Emily Purcell</a></h2> </div> <div class="shares shares-single" data-media="http://cdn.mamamia.com.au/wp/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/06160651/gill-pharaoh-post.jpg" data-url="http://www.mamamia.com.au/news/former-nurse-euthanasia" data-text="Healthy woman kills article-wrapper js-waypoint"> <div class="article-content"> <p class="leading-paragraph colour-news">Is euthanasia an acceptable solution to old age?</p> <p>At 75, Gill Pharaoh was healthy and enjoying life.</p> <p>She had a loving partner, two adult children and a <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/news/operation-safe-haven/" target="_blank">grandchild</a>. She was not <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/wellbeing/dear-melanoma-birthday-tears/" target="_blank">terminally ill</a>.</p> <p>But the former leading palliative care nurse decided to end her life in a Swiss suicide clinic anyway – because she couldn’t bear to grow old.</p> <figure id="attachment_508648" class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 730px"><img class="wp-image-508648 size-thumb-720" src="http://cdn.mamamia.com.au/wp/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/06160651/gill-pharaoh-post-720x547.jpg" alt="former nurse euthanasia" width="720" height="547"/><figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">Gill Pharaoh. Image by Julian Andrews via Twitter.</figcaption></figure> <p>Ms Pharaoh, who spent years nursing elderly people, previously said “growing old is awful” and she would rather euthanasia to becoming “an old lady hobbling up the road with a trolley”, the <em><a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-3182813/Healthy-former-nurse-75-died-Swiss-suicide-clinic-deciding-didn-t-want-risk-burden-family-NHS.html" target="_blank">Daily Mail</a></em> reports.</p> <p>She said her quality of life was deteriorating as she no longer cared for gardening or late dinner parties and suffered from tinnitus (ringing in the ears). The English woman said she was not prepared to go further downhill and did not want to be a burden on her children or the national health system.</p> <p>“I do not think old age is fun. I have gone just over the hill now. It is not going to start getting better,” she said.</p><?= display_ad('x18', 'hidden-xs hidden-md mm_incontent', 'MM In Content'); ?><?= display_ad('x20', 'visible-xs mm_mob_incontent', 'MM In Content (Mobile)'); ?> <p>Ms Pharaoh told some of her family and friends about her plans to kill herself before travelling to Zurich, Switzerland – one of the few countries with liberal assisted suicide laws – in late July with her partner of 25 years, John Southall.</p> <figure id="attachment_508652" class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 730px"><img class="wp-image-508652 size-thumb-720" src="http://cdn.mamamia.com.au/wp/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/06160753/John-and-Gill-post-720x547.jpg" alt="former nurse euthanasia" width="720" height="547"/><figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">Gill Pharaoh and her partner, John Southall. Image by Julian Andrews via Twitter.</figcaption></figure> <p>The night before her suicide, they wandered through the city and enjoyed a meal alongside the Rhine River, the <em><a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/health/11778859/Healthy-retired-nurse-ends-her-life-because-old-age-is-awful.html" target="_blank">Telegraph</a></em> reports.</p> <p>“The whole evening was very tranquil and enjoyable,” Mr Southall said.</p> <p>“I think it is what we both wanted. Gill had been thinking about it for years and I had no intention of spoiling it by getting emotional and heavy.”</p> <p>The next day, July 21, she made her final trip to an assisted suicide clinic in Basel.</p> <figure id="attachment_508653" class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 730px"><img class="wp-image-508653 size-thumb-720" src="http://cdn.mamamia.com.au/wp/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/06160806/john-southall-post-720x547.jpg" alt="former nurse euthanasia" width="720" height="547"/><figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">John Southall talks about his lost love. Image via Twitter.</figcaption></figure> <p>“Day by day I am enjoying my life. I simply do not want to follow this natural deterioration through to the last stage when I may be requiring a lot of help,” she wrote in her final blog post, in which she campaigns for the UK government to rethink their stance on euthanasia.</p> <p>“I feel my life is complete and I am ready to die.”</p> <p>Similarly, <a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/social/so-your-beliefs-about-euthanasia-are-clear-think-again/#yPk2zu71Ype7jfHL.99" target="_blank">Susan Potts</a> – a healthy, fit, 89-year-old Australian woman – shocked the nation in 2012, when she took her own life after receiving advice from ‘Doctor Death’ Philip Nitshke.</p> <p>The decision by both woman has caused huge debate around the world.</p> <p>While many people support euthanasia in certain circumstances, a person electing to die when they are physically fit and healthy considerably blurs the lines.</p> <p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-264298" src="http://cdn.mamamia.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/ytznmsxt-1390459594.jpg" alt="former nurse euthanasia" width="720" height="477"/></p> <p>Should someone have the right to deny themselves potentially years of quality life because they are done with living, when so many others would give anything to have just a few years more?</p> <p>When so many are suffering from painful, debilitating and terminal illnesses without access to euthanasia, is it fair that other, healthy people are being assisted in dying to avoid growing old?</p> <p>Ms Pharaoh said: “I want people to remember me as I am now – a bit worn around the edges and not quite at my peak, but still recognisably me!”</p> <p>Is that enough justification?</p> <blockquote><p><strong><em>For related stories, try these:</em></strong></p> <p><a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/news/philip-nitschke-deregistered/" target="_blank">He was a 45-year-old man without a terminal illness. But this euthanasia campaigner helped him to die.</a></p> <p><a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/wellbeing/euthanasia-should-an-individual-have-a-right-to-die/" target="_blank">Euthanasia: should an individual have the right to die?</a></p> <p><a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/wellbeing/dear-melanoma-birthday-tears/" target="_blank">‘I have terminal cancer. And I hate it when you tell me to “live in the moment”.’</a></p> <p><a href="http://www.mamamia.com.au/wellbeing/voluntary-euthanasia/" target="_blank">His wife died campaigning for voluntary euthanasia.
