On June 26, rainbow glitter littered the streets, boas were flung across shoulders, and tears of joy dripped off faces. There is no gray area.”</p> <p dir="ltr">But regardless of how the Indian government pushes anti-gay laws, Bollywood is pushing the envelope by casting many gay characters, Sakhuja said. But does Bollywood create immaculate expectations for relationships? Is Bollywood’s perception of love and timeline tantalizing with reality?</p> <p dir="ltr">The questions above truly opened my eyes to the actuality of “love,” and made me realize that I am probably not going to meet my soulmate dancing on a university lunch table with a fan blowing locks of my hair onto my face.</p> <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: center;"><strong>[Read Related: <a href="http://www.india.com/arts-and-culture/american-desis-podcast-explores-body-image-and-atheism-with-comedian-tushar-singh-486126/" target="_blank">‘American Desis’ Podcast Explores Body Image and Atheism with Comedian Tushar Singh]</a></strong></p> <p dir="ltr">The episode then advances to an in-depth conversation about growing up as a South Asian woman in America.</p> <p dir="ltr">Sakhuja begins by explaining her upbringing: “I pretended that I was Christian for a long time. I grew up with a unibrow and my mom was not okay with me getting my eyebrows threaded.”</p> <p dir="ltr">I couldn’t help but relate to nearly every point.
