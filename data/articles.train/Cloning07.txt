It’s capable of more than that, as it also doubles up as an access point, used to improve the performance of any router by adding an additional 5GHz wireless stream.</strong></em></span></p> <p>We’ve already seen excellent quad-stream 4&#215;4 MIMO 802.11ac performance from the <a href="http://www.kitguru.net/networking/routers/zardon/asus-rt-ac87u-4x4-bridge-mode-review/" target="_blank">Asus RT-AC87U</a> and Netgear has the Nighthawk X4 R7500, which offers a similar quad-stream 802.11ac set up.</p> <p>There aren’t exactly a lot of devices out there which support quad-stream 802.11ac though, so the Asus EA-AC87 4&#215;4 wireless bridge is of particular interest as it’s one way to get those top speeds from your new router.<br /> <a href="http://www.kitguru.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/asus-bridge.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/downloads/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/asus-bridge.jpg');"  rel="lightbox[260340]" title="Asus EA-AC87 4x4 wireless bridge review"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-260344" src="http://www.kitguru.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/asus-bridge.jpg" alt="asus bridge" width="650" height="470" /></a></p> <p>A network bridge is an inherently simple device, designed to extend the range of a wireless signal. If you’ve ever logged on to a wireless hotspot in a coffee shop or elsewhere, you’ve used one before.</p> <p>Usually, they work by cloning the details from the main router’s software, including its security password and SSID name. IP addresses are handed out from a pool taken from its DHCP server, with identical port forwarding rules and so on. To the user, the bridge is invisible, since all they ever see is a single wireless network.</p> <p>They’re essential in large buildings that offer site-wide wi-fi, such as hotels and campuses. Likewise, they’re one option for improved wireless speeds in homes.</p> <p>The Asus EA-AC87 works as a wireless bridge for 5GHz 802.11n or 802.11ac networks. With a look that is best described as like a small bug, not unlike one of the robots from games like Supreme Commander or Total Annihilation, it uniquely has four antennas, so it can be used to extend a 4&#215;4 802.11ac signal from a router such as the RT-AC87U. With four wireless streams of 433 Mbps each, the EA-AC87 can handle around 1733 Mbps over 802.11ac.</p> <p>It also has five ethernet ports on it for connecting wired local clients, effectively acting as a giant ethernet dongle for them.</p> <p>By flipping a switch at the back, like other wireless bridges, the EA-AC87 can be used as an access point, with a single ethernet cable linking it with a router to improve wireless performance. Again, it’s capable of quad-stream 802.11ac speeds in this mode, but with a separate wireless network and SSID.</p> <p><em>Asus EA-AC87 features:</em></p> <ul> <li>World’s fastest 5 GHz data rate of performance and wireless signal coverage up to 465 m2(around 5,000 ft2).</li> <li>Media Bridge mode connects any wired device to a wireless network.</li> <li>Access Point mode upgrades old routers to
