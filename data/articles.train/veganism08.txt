Having chili in the pantry is great for those days when you want a chili dog, or a quick lunch for the kids.<br /> <br /> INGREDIENTS<br /> <i>Makes seven 1 quart/litre jars</i><br /> - 1.5 cups dry black beans<br /> - 1.5 cups dry red kidney beans<br /> <br /> - 1/4 cup oil<br /> - 4 cups chopped onion<br /> - 3 cups chopped green pepper<br /> - 3/4 cup chopped garlic<br /> - 1/2 cup chili powder<br /> - 1/4 cup oregano<br /> - 2 tbsp cumin<br /> - 1/2 tsp ground cloves<br /> - 1 tbsp salt<br /> - 2 cups brown lentils<br /> - 1 48oz can tomato juice<br /> - 2 28oz cans chopped tomatoes, with juice<br /> - 4 cups water<br /> - 1/2 cup sugar<br /> <br /> METHOD<br /> 1. Soak beans overnight in plenty of water. Drain and rinse the next day.<br /> 2. Get all of your canning apparatus ready (canner, jars, lids). <br /> 3. In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Sauté onion, green pepper, and garlic for 5-7 mins, or until translucent. Add spices and salt and sauté for a minute, until fragrant.<br /> 4. Add the remaining ingredients and mix well. Increase heat to med-hi and bring to bubbling, stirring regularly.<br /> 5. Transfer chili to sterilised jars, making sure each one has the same ratio of solid to liquid. Put on the lids and tighten the rings finger tight.<br /> 6. Process in the pressure canner at 10lbs of pressure for 75 mins. Remove from heat and let pressure drop to zero.
