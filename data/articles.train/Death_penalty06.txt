                <h1 class="entry-title">James Holmes jury to announce sentence: Death or life in prison</h1>                                                 <aside id="meta-211032" class="entry-meta"><span class="posted-on">Posted on </span><a href="http://gantdaily.com/2015/08/07/james-holmes-jury-to-announce-sentence-death-or-life-in-prison/" title="5:15 PM" class="post-time"><time class="entry-date updated" datetime="2015-08-07T17:15:04+00:00">Friday, August 7, 2015</time></a><span class="by-author"> by </span><span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://gantdaily.com/author/cnn/" title="View all posts by CNN" rel="author">CNN</a></span><span class="posted-in"> in </span><a href="http://gantdaily.com/category/news/national-news-news/" rel="tag">National News</a> 		</aside>                                         </header>                 <div class="entry-content"> 					<p>A jury will announce its verdict Friday on whether James Holmes should be executed or spend life in prison for committing mass murder in a Colorado movie theater that became one of the worst shootings in U.S. history.</p> <p>The jury reached a sentencing verdict after deliberating almost seven hours since late Thursday, and the court will announce the jury&#8217;s sentence at 7 p.m. ET.</p> <p>Holmes killed 12 people and wounded 70 more in a movie theater in the Denver suburb of Aurora in July 2012. He was convicted last month of 24 counts of first-degree murder &#8212; two for each of the victims slain in the shooting. The convictions initiated proceedings on whether he should receive the death penalty.</p> <p>In Friday&#8217;s deliberations, the jury asked the court if it could view a 45-minute video of the crime scene taken by a police investigator.</p> <p>Attorneys for Holmes objected, saying the video was too graphic.</p> <p>But the judge ruled the jury could use a TV monitor and DVD player for only 50 minutes to play the video just once. The video isn&#8217;t unfairly prejudicial, has probative value, and has already been admitted into evidence during the trial, the judge ruled.</p> <p>The video, shown earlier in the trial, provides a tour of the massacre site where bodies are sprawled on the floor, aisle after aisle. Bodies lay contorted, trying to hide, and curled in fetal positions. Bullets, a rifle, soda cups, and popcorn litter the floor. </p> <p>Though the public has had access to the trial through a court-controlled closed-circuit camera, the crime scene video was shown only to the jury and the gallery and hasn&#8217;t been distributed publicly.</p> <p>The same jury earlier convicted 27-year-old Holmes for the capital murder of 12 people. The jury also convicted him of attempted murder in the wounding of 70 more people.</p> <p>The shooting occurred during a midnight showing of a Batman movie, &#8220;The Dark Knight Rises,&#8221; when Holmes burst into the theater through a rear door and opened fire.</p> <p>Holmes&#8217; attorneys argued that their client is mentally ill and suffered a psychotic episode when he colored his hair orange-red like the villain The Joker, rigged his apartment with explosives, dressed in tactical gear, and sprayed bullets into the theater under a cloak of darkness and tear gas.</p> <p>In closing arguments Thursday, public defender Tamara Brady said the assault was a &#8220;tragedy&#8221; born of disease, not choice. The deaths cannot be answered by another death, and Holmes would be punished for the remainder of his days under a life sentence, she said.</p> <p>Prosecutors, however, argued that Holmes should die for his crimes. His mental illness didn&#8217;t prevent him from acting &#8220;rationally&#8221; elsewhere in his life, and the defendant must be held accountable for the mass murder, prosecutors said.</p> <p>For Holmes, justice is his execution, District Attorney George Brauchler told the jury.</p> <p>Holmes admitted to his crimes and had pleaded not guilty by reason of insanity. </p> <p>But the jury rejected that defense in July, convicting him of a total of 165 counts, including the 24 charges of first-degree murder.</p> <p>In the shooting, Holmes bought a ticket to see the Batman movie 12 days prior to the assault.</p> <p>At the time, Holmes reportedly held a &#8220;human capital&#8221; credo that killing people would add value to his life. &#8220;You take away life, and your human capital is limitless,&#8221; Holmes said in a chat message to his then-girlfriend months before the massacre.</p> <p>If people were injured or maimed in the shooting, they amounted to &#8220;collateral damage,&#8221; Holmes told a court-appointed psychiatrist after the shootings.</p> <p>Like other patrons on July 19, 2012, Holmes walked into the theater No. 9 of Century Aurora 16 Multiplex Theater. </p> <p>He then walked out a rear door, which he left propped open.</p> <p>Just after midnight, about 18 minutes into the movie, he returned wearing a ballistic helmet, a gas mask, black gloves, and protective gear for his legs, throat and groin.</p> <p>A tear gas canister exploded in the theater, then gunfire rained from an AR-15 rifle, a 12-gauge shotgun and at least one .40 caliber handgun. </p> <p>Holmes fled. But he was arrested in the parking lot outside the theater on July 20, 2012, about seven minutes after the first 911 calls were made to police.</p> <div class="wp-post-navigation"> 									   <div
