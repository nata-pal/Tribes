Summer Reads: Book Reviews

in Arts and Entertainment, Books, Uncategorized Leave a comment

As you are lazing around the pools and beaches this summer, it’s not always easy to find that perfect gay novel that will titillate your mind and imagination. These summer reads will have you flipping pages poolside no matter where your interests lie.

William of the Tafel
Exploring Post Apocalyptic Utopian Earth Where Gender Plays No Role

Author Hans M. Hirschi’s new LGBT novel is set 500 years into the future on a post apocalyptic Earth where the few people that remain have taken refuge underground beneath a mountain. The main character, seventeen-year-old Willem, must navigate through a battered world that is the result of our generation’s carelessness. Luckily he meets Hery, another young man, who helps him along the path to his destiny.

In their meeting, we learn how in this futuristic world, people have evolved to look beyond racial and gender lines. (At least most have; those that haven’t are doomed to pay the price for holding on to twenty-first century ideology.) That Willem and Hery decide to become a union – or ‘to mate’, as described in the book – stirs no reaction from the other characters in the novel. 500 years into the future, homosexuality has become completely accepted.

Bohunk’s Big To-Do
An Unexpected Love Triangle Between a Go-Go Boy, His Boyfriend, and His Mother

Julian Jones’ new novel evokes some of the elements of Southern Gothic fiction within a Midwestern setting. Bo Mickey, a 22-yearold go-go boy tends to be a little selfish, but is also tender-hearted. He is hurting from the recent marriage of his boyfriend to his mother. Will he figure out a way to resolve his twisted love triangle in time to find that honorable solution?

Thematically, Bohunk’s Big To-Do is about moral conscience: how susceptible we are to discarding our values under particular influence or provocation, how we sometimes seek reasons to justify betraying our conscience, and ultimately how intrinsic personal pride is who we are and who we are destined to become.

The Holy Mark
The Tragedy of a Fallen Priest Leaving You to Ponder Good Vs. Evil

Gregory Alexander has created a haunting and poignant tale of Father Tony, who has led his whole life knowing that he would become a priest. At birth his immigrant grandmother declared that his very particular birthmark was a sign from God that he should become a priest. Because of his preordained life path, Tony foregoes many opportunities; in love and in life.

Those marked by God, though, are often marked by men as well: Father Tony’s jealous uncle will never forgive him for finding favor with the Miggliore matriarch. And with his ties to the city’s Catholic hierarchy, he’ll plot to destroy his nephew if it takes the rest of his life. Meanwhile Father Tony is determined to outwit his uncle and the Church, even if he has to conceal his identity and prowl the streets of New Orleans by night to do it.
