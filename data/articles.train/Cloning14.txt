 <h2 class="hide">Bulletproof Mac Backup: 5 Apps to Safeguard Your Data</h2>  <p>You can beef up the security on your Mac all you want, but all the firewalls and antivirus apps in the world mean nothing when that can of soda tips over on your Macbook, destroying your laptop and all its data forever.</p><p>You may have some of your data backed up to a cloud service, but that can't do much to restore settings, shortcuts, and passwords stored on your keychain. For that type of restoration, you'd need a back up app that can make a complete copy of your system. Of course, it's a pain in the ass to find the right app when there are so many that claim to do the same things.</p><p>To help save you the time it would take to research all of the apps out there, here is my list of the 5 best back up apps available for Mac OS X.</p> <figure class="whtGallery" id="64280253docPartGal880027" role="group"><div class="gallery-layout"><div class="gallery-layout-container"><figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0"><div id="docPartGal8800270"><object width="532" height="324"><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><param name="movie" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/zGenLch1wcU?version=3&amp;autoplay=0&amp;rel=0&amp;vq=2&amp;fs=1&amp;modestbranding=1" /><param name="allowscriptaccess" value="never" /><param name="wmode" value="transparent" /><embed src="http://www.youtube.com/v/zGenLch1wcU?version=3&amp;autoplay=0&amp;rel=0&amp;vq=2&amp;fs=1&amp;modestbranding=1" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" wmode="transparent" width="532" height="324" allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="never"></embed><noscript>Please enable JavaScript to watch this video.</noscript></object></div><script type="text/javascript">//<![CDATA[ document.getElementById("docPartGal8800270").innerHTML = 'Video Loading'; //]]></script></figure></div></div></figure>  <aside class="aph_rads"><div id="videorads" class="clearfix" style="margin-top:-20px;overflow:hidden;height:2em;"></div></aside> <p><em>Some of these apps require you to <a href="http://mac-how-to.wonderhowto.com/how-to/open-third-party-apps-from-unidentified-developers-mac-os-x-0158095/">enable apps form unidentified developers in your security settings</a>, so be sure to do that before trying any of them out.</em></p></section> <section> <h2 class="sectionHeadline">#1. Disk Utility</h2><p>You may have thought the built-in <strong><a href="http://tag.wonderhowto.com/disk-utility/">Disk Utility</a></strong> app was only good for reformatting USB and external drives and <a href="http://mac-how-to.wonderhowto.com/how-to/dual-boot-mac-os-x-10-11-el-capitan-10-10-yosemite-0162372/">partitioning your internal drive</a>, but it also comes with a decent cloning tool as well. Compared to other apps in this guide, Disk Utility is lacking a lot of features, like scheduled backups and blacklisting certain folders, but if you are looking for a simple and free solution, this is for you.</p> <figure class="whtGallery" id="40095770docPartGal880005" role="group"><div class="gallery-layout"><div class="gallery-layout-container"><figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0"><img src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/00/73/63573100060020/0/bulletproof-mac-backup-5-apps-safeguard-your-data.w654.jpg" alt="Bulletproof Mac Backup: 5 Apps to Safeguard Your Data" width="532" height="532" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;"/></figure></div></div></figure> <p>To create a back up, all you have to do is open Disk Utility, select your Mac's hard drive from the left hand pane, click on the <strong>Restore</strong> tab, and then drag your external hard drive or USB to the <strong>Destination</strong> field. Once you click on Restore, Disk Utility will begin to clone your drive, creating a fully bootable copy of your Mac's hard drive.</p></section> <section> <h2 class="sectionHeadline">#2. Time Machine</h2><p>Every Mac also ships with <strong><a href="http://tag.wonderhowto.com/time-machine-mac/">Time Machine</a></strong>, which is Apple's backup solution. Just like with most of Apple's products, they tried to make the entire process as simple as possible.</p> <figure class="whtGallery" id="3757534docPartGal880009" role="group"><div class="gallery-layout"><div class="gallery-layout-container"><figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0"><img src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/52/86/63573100080332/0/bulletproof-mac-backup-5-apps-safeguard-your-data.w654.jpg" alt="Bulletproof Mac Backup: 5 Apps to Safeguard Your Data" width="532" height="532" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;"/></figure></div></div></figure> <p>Setting up an external hard drive or AirPort Time Capsule with Time Machine is as simple as plugging it in or having your Mac detect it on your network. Once Time Machine has been turned on, it will automatically back up modified files and let you know of its progress though its menu bar icon.</p> <figure class="whtGallery" id="12852874docPartGal880011" role="group"><div class="gallery-layout"><div class="gallery-layout-container"><figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0"><img src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/77/17/63573098420238/0/bulletproof-mac-backup-5-apps-safeguard-your-data.w654.jpg" alt="Bulletproof Mac Backup: 5 Apps to Safeguard Your Data" width="532" height="532" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;"/></figure></div></div></figure>  <p class="caption">Time Machine Restore</p><small>Image via <a href="http://www.shirt-pocket.com/downloads/SuperDuper!.dmg" rel="nofollow">Apple Support</a></small> <p>Restoring files is just as easy. When you start up a new or factory-restored Mac, you will have the option to restore files from a Time Machine backup. However, if you'd rather restore a single file or folder from an earlier date, you can do so by selecting <strong>Enter Time Machine</strong> from the Time Machine menu while in Finder. A timeline of all the backed up copies will then pop up with the option to restore your desired version.</p></section> <section> <h2 class="sectionHeadline">#3. SuperDuper!</h2><p><a  href="http://www.shirt-pocket.com/SuperDuper/SuperDuperDescription.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" >SuperDuper! by Shirt Pocket</a> is a freemium app that comes with basic drive cloning abilities that will allow you to create bootable copies of your Mac's hard drive.
