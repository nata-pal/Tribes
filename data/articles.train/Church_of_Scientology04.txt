               <p>Do you love Tom Cruise?</p> <p>  <p>The way he runs with jacked intensity, that single wisp of un-gelled hair bobbing to and fro in the breeze? That foolhardy smile that says, &#8220;Sorry, boss, my way or the highway,&#8221; with a combination of youthful na&#239;vety and the cocksureness of a born winner? His compact, buff as hell little body that defies age and time? The way his movies are feverishly erected around him like cardboard boxes&#160;that, in his presence, morph into rocket ships?&#160;</p> <p>  <p>Well, if so, enjoy this naked sculpture of the man, the legend, the Cruise, courtesy of celeb-crazed artist <a href="http://www.cacanet.com/daniel-edwards.html" target="_blank">Daniel Edwards</a>.</p> <p>The sculpture, a nude shroud of Cruise himself, serves as a tribute to the actor&#8217;s 25th year with the Church of Scientology &#8212; <a href="http://www.esquire.com/entertainment/movies/news/a36209/tom-cruise-leaving-scientology/" target="_blank">what could be&#160;his last</a>. Sculptor Edwards and gallery Cory Allen Contemporary Art organized a &#8220;pop-up Church of Scientology&#8221; in honor of the anniversary, to be&#160;assembled near the authentic Church&#8217;s Clearwater headquarters in Florida.</p> <p>  <p>Taking a hint from the Shroud of Turin, the Shroud of Scientology is a 14-foot rectangular beast, depicting a <em>fully</em> groomed Cruise clutching the Scientology cross on his chest.&#160;</p> <p>  <p>&#8220;Radiocarbon dating will never rule out the Shroud of Scientology&#8217;s authenticity,&#8221; &#160;<span class="il">Edwards explained in a statement</span>. &#8220;It exists as a document of Tom Cruise&#8217;s faith in Scientology &#8212; a photo negative of the radiance of his soul. It gives evidence for future generations that Tom Cruise not only belonged to Scientology, but saved it from obscurity.&#8221;</p> <p>Also on display is a Church of Scientology Silver Anniversary Medal for Tom Cruise.&#160;</p> <p>  <p>Edwards and Cory Allen are known for their controversial Hollywood-centric work &#8212; Edwards, thanks to his sculptures of stars including&#160;<a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/05/29/baby-kimye-and-the-royal-heir-artist-daniel-edwards-goes-there-photos_n_3351386.html" target="_blank">North West</a>&#160;and <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/08/10/daniel-edwards-bieber-gomez-nude_n_923350.html" target="_blank">Justin Bieber</a>, and, in 2006, a bronze sculpture of <a href="http://www.caplakesting.com/DE_SURI_CATALOG.pdf" target="_blank">Suri Cruise&#8217;s &#8220;first bowel movement.&#8221;</a>&#160;Cory Allen, on the other hand, had the laughably bad idea to put <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/09/04/jennifer-lawrence-nudes-art-_n_5766972.html" target="_blank">Jennifer Lawrence&#8217;s leaked nudes</a>&#160;on display as art.</p> <p>  <p>Despite the artists&#8217; less-than-perfect track record, we can&#8217;t deny our excitement at a Cruise-centric work, especially one in the buff. Flashback to P.T. Anderson&#8217;s wise words on the set of &#8220;Magnolia&#8221;: &#8220;<a href="http://grantland.com/features/tom-cruise-magnolia-amy-nicholson/" target="_blank">Tom Cruise is the biggest movie star in the world. Are you kidding? Of course he&#8217;s got the world&#8217;s biggest cock.</a>&#8220;</p> <p>  <p><em>The &#8216;Pop-up Church of Scientology&#8217; featuring the &#8220;Shroud of Scientology&#8221; will open to the public at Cory Allen Contemporary Art&#8217;s The Showroom, located in the Warehouse Arts District in St. Petersburg, FL, on <span class="aBn"><span class="aQJ">August 8,
