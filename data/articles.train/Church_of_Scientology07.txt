The Christian life runs the full range of emotions: joy, peace, delight, love, sorrow, grief, concern, etc.&nbsp; Ours is a faith not only of the head but also of the heart.&nbsp; As a result it is right and proper to desire spiritual experience.&nbsp; The problem is that many Christians cannot tell the difference between enthusiasm for God and manipulation of the moment.&nbsp; Entertainment can look strangely like worship; fun can masquerade as joy; fleshly excitement can be perceived as divine encounter.</span></span></div> <div style="color: #0433ff; min-height: 13px;"> <span style="font-family: Helvetica Neue, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="letter-spacing: 0.0px;"></span><br /></span></div> <div style="color: #0433ff;"> <span style="letter-spacing: 0.0px;"><span style="font-family: Helvetica Neue, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Part of our problem today is that out of the free-love (that is, drugs and sex) revolution of the 1960s has sprung an insatiable desire for experience.&nbsp; Experience has mounted the throne and barks out orders to a doting constituency that has lost patience in a world that does not make sense.&nbsp; If we cannot understand life, if in fact life makes no sense, at least we can enjoy ourselves.&nbsp; If it feels good, it may not be right, but it is better than nothing.</span></span></div> <div style="color: #0433ff; min-height: 13px;"> <span style="font-family: Helvetica Neue, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="letter-spacing: 0.0px;"></span><br /></span></div> <div style="color: #0433ff;"> <span style="letter-spacing: 0.0px;"><span style="font-family: Helvetica Neue, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">This attitude &#8212; called postmodernism by those who like to coin phrases &#8212; has also crept into the church.&nbsp; Christians want an experience that makes them feel good.&nbsp; So dominating has this desire become that truth has increasingly taken a back seat to a good time.</span></span></div> <div style="color: #0433ff; min-height: 13px;"> <span style="font-family: Helvetica Neue, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="letter-spacing: 0.0px;"></span><br /></span></div> <div style="color: #0433ff;"> <span style="letter-spacing: 0.0px;"><span style="font-family: Helvetica Neue, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">We should be ever mindful that the Word &#8212; not experience &#8212; is our authority.&nbsp; True delight in God should emerge from biblical truth.&nbsp; Next we should take a good look at the Psalms.&nbsp; There we find the writers absolutely in love with and excited about God.&nbsp; Psalm 36:7-9, for example, reads:</span></span></div> <div style="color: #ff2600;"> <span style="font-family: Helvetica Neue, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="letter-spacing: 0.0px;">&#8220;How precious is Your loving-kindness, O God!&nbsp; Therefore the children of men put their trust under the shadow of Your wings.&nbsp; They are abundantly satisfied with the fullness of Your house, and You give them drink from the river of Your pleasures.&nbsp; For with You is the fountain of life; in Your light we see light.</span><span style="color: #0433ff; letter-spacing: 0.0px;">&#8221;</span></span></div> <div style="color: #0433ff;"> <span style="letter-spacing: 0.0px;"><span style="font-family: Helvetica Neue, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /> Here is a man (David) finding great joy in his Lord.&nbsp; He is not wrapped up in the side issues; he is not drumming up feelings; he is not being whipped into a mood.&nbsp; He is simply reflecting on his God and his heart can hardly contain what it views.&nbsp; This is the spiritual experience we should crave.</span></span></div> <div style="min-height: 13px;"> <span style="font-family: Helvetica Neue, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="letter-spacing: 0.0px;"></span><br /></span></div> <div style="min-height: 13px;"> <span style="font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;
