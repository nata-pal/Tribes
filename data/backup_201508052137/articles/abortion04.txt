href="http://dcabortionfund.org/2015/07/yup-this-is-a-play-about-abortion/" title="Permanent link to Yup. This is a play about abortion." rel="bookmark" rev="post-2174">Yup. This is a play about abortion.</a></h1> 	 	<div class="entry-content full-content"> <p><em><a href="http://dcabortionfund.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/oneinthree.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2175" src="http://dcabortionfund.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/oneinthree-300x168.jpg" alt="oneinthree" width="300" height="168" /></a>This week&#8217;s blog is a guest post from Rachel Cooke, associate director of communications at Advocates for Youth. Advocates for Youth&#8217;s 1 in 3 Campaign is part of the Capital Fringe festival this year with <a href="https://www.capitalfringe.org/events/550-out-of-silence-abortion-stories-from-the-1-in-3-campaign">Out of Silence: Abortion Stories from the 1 in 3 Campaign</a>. The DC Abortion Fund is glad to support their effort, and we hope we&#8217;ll see you at the show.</em></p> <p>There is no doubt that the arts play an important role in how people understand their world and the issues that affect those around them. In particular, theater has often been used to bring humanity back to complicated issues, to remind us that beyond the drama and conflict, there are personal lives at stake.  Whether it is <i>Angels in America, Kiss of the Spiderwoman, </i>or <i>A Raisin in the </i><em>Sun</em>, theater peels back the layers, it gives us a new vocabulary, and plants a seed for change. We’re big fans of the arts at the 1 in 3 Campaign; some of you might have seen our <a href="https://www.facebook.com/1in3Stories/photos/pb.178641292290604.-2207520000.1435866032./440901866064544/?type=3&amp;theater">valentines</a>, <a href="http://www.1in3campaign.org/1in3weeksp15">pop-up stickers</a> or abortion provider <a href="http://1in3.tumblr.com/image/113278641653">thank-you images</a> over the years.</p> <p>The <a href="http://www.1in3campaign.org">1 in 3 Campaign</a>, a project of Advocates for Youth, is a grassroots movement to de-stigmatize the experience of people who have had abortions and empower those who believe in safe and legal access to abortion care to take a stand. In the past four years we have been honored with over 800 written or video stories of abortion and in those stories we have found community. Built on the success of prior social change movements, we’re putting real-life stories back where they need to be: at the center of a new national dialogue about abortion, one steeped in empathy and demonstrative of the importance abortion care has played in the lives of so many across generations.</p> <p>Sharing our stories is an important tool in strengthening support for abortion care nationwide, but how do you get more people to hear these stories? The play’s the thing!</p> <p>Based on a number of the real stories submitted to the campaign, and written by a veritable who’s who of DC female playwrights , we commissioned <i>Out of Silence</i> to stage, to elevate real stories of experiences with abortion. The short vignettes in the play are funny, heartbreaking, inspiring and messy but always thought-provoking and honest. We are so excited for the show to be part of the <a href="https://www.capitalfringe.org/events/550-out-of-silence-abortion-stories-from-the-1-in-3-campaign">Capital Fringe Festival</a> this July.</p> <p>Even though each vignette tells a unique story, what is most remarkable is how the need for safe, accessible and affordable abortion care is a common thread that runs throughout the play. We at Advocates believe that reproductive justice is inextricably linked to economic justice. Too many women, especially young women, are struggling to access the care they need and have a right to. We stand with our friends and allies like <a href="http://dcabortionfund.org/">DC Abortion Fund</a> who work to make reproductive justice a reality for all.</p> <p><em>Post by Rachel Cooke, image courtesy 1 in 3 Campaign.</em></p> 		<div class="clear"></div> 	</div><!-- .entry-content--> 	    	 	</div><!-- .post --><div class="rule"><hr /></div> <div id="post-content-2169" class="post-2169 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-dcaf-volunteers full"> 	 	<h1 class="entry-title full-title"><a href="http://dcabortionfund.org/2015/06/sustaining-donors-the-charms-that-make-dcaf-sparkle/" title="Permanent link to Sustaining donors: The charms that make DCAF sparkle" rel="bookmark" rev="post-2169">Sustaining donors: The charms that make DCAF sparkle</a></h1> 	 	<div class="entry-content full-content"> <div id="attachment_2170" style="width: 305px" class="wp-caption alignleft"><a href="http://dcabortionfund.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/FullSizeRender.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-2170" src="http://dcabortionfund.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/FullSizeRender-295x300.jpg" alt="Monthly donors get a coat hanger charm to show they are #charmedbyDCAF. " width="295" height="300" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Monthly donors get a coat hanger charm to show they are #charmedbyDCAF.</p></div> <p><em>What keeps DCAF afloat? Our incredible sustaining donors. Volunteer Carrie E. talks about what makes these donors so special. </em></p> <p>In 2014 alone, the DC Abortion Fund pledged $198,889 on behalf of 950 pregnant patients. The average pledge was $231 per patient.</p> <p>So where does this money come from? Well, a lot comes from grants and our fundraising events. It also comes from our wonderful sustaining donors, whose contributions make up about a quarter of our 2015 budget.</p> <p>Our sustaining donors are those who have committed to giving a set amount to DCAF each month. It doesn’t have to be a large chunk of your paycheck — even $10 a month is enough to make you part of the program.</p> <p>I became a sustaining donor after my first week as a case manager trainee. After speaking with a just a handful of the scared, stressed, and strapped-for-cash patients that we work with, and seeing just how expensive some abortion procedures can be, I realized that even a small amount of money could make a difference. I probably didn’t need as many Starbucks runs as I took anyway—and when I see the money taken out of my account every month, I do feel the warm and fuzzies of helping someone else.</p> <p>In addition to that happy feeling of helping out someone who needs it, you will receive a pretty badass coat hanger charm as a gift for becoming a sustaining donor. As you may have heard, some conservative pundits misunderstood the meaning of this charm, and went on the offensive in 2014. They missed the point completely: The coat hanger symbolizes what can happen when women do not have access to abortion care. Unfortunately for them and very fortunately for us, we got a ton of new donors as a result of their misplaced anger. Sorry, Sarah Palin.</p> <p>&#8220;Our monthly donors are responsible for so much of our success,&#8221; said DCAF Treasurer Emily C. &#8220;They allow us to plan on our budget and subsequently serve our patients more effectively.&#8221;</p> <p><strong><a href="http://dcabortionfund.org/donate/">If you’re interested in becoming a sustaining donor, or to just donate a little when you can, learn more here</a>.</strong></p> <p><em>By volunteer Carrie E., photo by Lindsey W. </em><br /> <strong><em><br /> </em></strong></p> 		<div class="clear"></div> 	</div><!-- .entry-content--> 	    	 	</div><!-- .post --><div class="rule"><hr /></div> <div class="pagination"> 	<span class="previous"></span> 	<span class="next"><a href="http://dcabortionfund.org/page/2/" >Next &raquo;</a></span> </div></div><!--#content-->  <hr class="lofi" /> <div id="sidebar"> 	 <div id="carrington-subscribe" class="widget"> 	<table width="350" border="0">   <tr>     <td width="212"><div align="left"><a class="feed alignleft" href="https://salsa3.salsalabs.com/o/2082/donate_page/donatenow"><img src="http://dcabortionfund.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/DCAF-Donate-Now.jpg" border="0" alt="DonateNow" /></a></div></td>     <td width="138"><div align="right"><a class="feed alignright" title="RSS 2.0 feed for posts" rel="alternate" href="http://dcabortionfund.org/feed/"><img src="http://dcabortionfund.org/wp-content/themes/carrington-blog/img/rss-button.gif" alt="The DC Abortion Fund latest posts" title="The DC Abortion Fund latest posts" />     </a></div></td>   </tr> </table></div><!--.widget--> 	<div id="carrington-about" class="widget"> 		<div class="about"> 			<h2 class="widget-title">About The DC Abortion Fund</h2> <p>DC Abortion Fund (DCAF) is an all-volunteer nonprofit that makes grants to women in DC, Maryland, and Virginia who cannot afford the full cost of an abortion.
