											<h1 class="entry-title">Protect All Human Life</h1> 					 					<div class="entry-content"> 						 <!-- Facebook Like Button v1.9.6 BEGIN [http://blog.bottomlessinc.com] --> <iframe src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.ronpaul.com%2Fon-the-issues%2Fabortion%2F&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=false&amp;width=450&amp;action=like&amp;colorscheme=light" scrolling="no" frameborder="0" allowTransparency="true" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:450px; height: 30px; align: left; margin: 2px 0px 2px 0px"></iframe> <!-- Facebook Like Button END --> <p>The heated debate about abortion is filled with emotional arguments that usually center on considerations such as sexual morality, religious beliefs, women&#8217;s rights, or purely on pragmatic reasons: if abortion were made illegal it would still take place &#8211; under unsanitary conditions that would endanger additional lives.</p> <p>However, a rational evaluation of abortion must be built upon one single question: <strong>When exactly does human life begin? At conception, at birth or somewhere in between?</strong></p> <p>Not even the most radical feminist would find it okay to tear apart a recently-born baby just because it is not wanted by its mother. All other considerations aside, the only reason many individuals can support abortion with a good conscience is because they believe it&#8217;s not murder&#8230; and that unborn babies do not count as human beings.</p> <p><a href="http://www.ronpaul.com" >Ron Paul</a> has delivered more than 4,000 babies. He believes that human life starts at conception, and that casual elimination of the unborn leads to a careless attitude towards all life.</p> <p>Recalling his personal observation of a late-term abortion performed by one of his instructors during his medical residency, Ron Paul stated, <em>&#8220;It was pretty dramatic for me to see a two-and-a-half-pound baby taken out crying and breathing and put in a bucket.&#8221;</em></p> <p>In an Oct. 27, 1999 speech to Congress, Ron Paul said:</p> <blockquote><p>&#8220;I am strongly pro-life. I think one of the most disastrous rulings of this century was Roe versus Wade. I do believe in the slippery slope theory. I believe that if people are careless and casual about life at the beginning of life, we will be careless and casual about life at the end. Abortion leads to euthanasia. I believe that.&#8221;</p></blockquote> <p>During a May 15, 2007, appearance on the Fox News talk show <em>Hannity and Colmes</em>, Ron Paul argued that his pro-life position was consistent with his libertarian values, asking, <em>&#8220;If you can&#8217;t protect life then how can you protect liberty?&#8221;</em> Additionally, Ron Paul said that since he believes libertarians support non-aggression, libertarians should oppose abortion because abortion is <em>&#8220;an act of aggression&#8221;</em> against a fetus.</p> <p>At the GOP Values Voter Presidential Debate on Sep 17, 2007, Ron Paul was asked what he will do to restore legal protection to the unborn:</p> <blockquote><p>&#8220;As an O.B. doctor of thirty years, and having delivered 4,000 babies, I can assure you life begins at conception. I am legally responsible for the unborn, no matter what I do, so there&#8217;s a legal life there. The unborn has inheritance rights, and if there&#8217;s an injury or a killing, there is a legal entity. There is no doubt about it.&#8221;</p></blockquote> <p>At the GOP YouTube debate in St. Petersburg, Florida, on Nov 28, 2007, Ron Paul was asked what a woman would be charged with if abortion becomes illegal and she obtains an abortion anyway:</p> <blockquote><p>&#8220;The first thing we have to do is get the federal government out of it. We don&#8217;t need a federal abortion police. That&#8217;s the last thing that we need. There has to be a criminal penalty for the person that&#8217;s committing that crime. And I think that is the abortionist. As for the punishment, I don&#8217;t think that should be up to the president to decide.&#8221;</p></blockquote> <p>For many years, Ron Paul has been speaking up for babies&#8217; rights. He passionately defends those who cannot speak for themselves because they haven&#8217;t been born yet.</p> <p>In order to <em>&#8220;offset the effects of Roe v. Wade&#8221;</em>, Paul voted in favor of the federal Partial-Birth Abortion Ban Act of 2003. He has described partial birth abortion as a <em>&#8220;barbaric procedure&#8221;</em>.</p> <p>At the same time, Ron Paul believes that the ninth and tenth amendments to the U.S. Constitution do not grant the federal government any authority to legalize or ban abortion. Instead, it is up to the individual states to prohibit abortion.</p> <p>Many people feel very strongly about the issue of abortion, and once they make up their minds they rarely change their opinion. If you are undecided and/or open-minded, check out <a href="http://www.nrlc.org/abortion/pba/diagram.html" target="_BLANK">this page</a> and <a href="http://www.priestsforlife.org/images/index.htm" target="_BLANK">this site</a> for more information about abortion, including images and a description of medical procedures.</p> <div class='shareaholic-canvas' data-app-id='51579' data-app='share_buttons' data-title='Protect All Human Life' data-link='http://www.ronpaul.com/on-the-issues/abortion/' data-summary=''></div><div class="g g-2"><div class="g-single a-14"><script async src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js"></script> <!-- rp top leaderboard 2015 --> <ins class="adsbygoogle"      style="display:inline-block;width:728px;height:90px"      data-ad-client="ca-pub-3666212842414688"      data-ad-slot="9478233584"></ins> <script> (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({}); </script></div></div><div class='yarpp-related'> <h3>Related posts:</h3><ol> <li><a href="http://www.ronpaul.com/misc/books/ron-paul-a-life-of-ideas/" rel="bookmark" title="Ron Paul: A Life of Ideas">Ron Paul: A Life of Ideas </a></li> <li><a href="http://www.ronpaul.com/on-the-issues/" rel="bookmark" title="Issues">Issues </a></li> <li><a href="http://www.ronpaul.com/who-is-ron-paul/" rel="bookmark" title="Who Is Ron Paul?">Who Is Ron Paul?
